ISSUES IN THE NUCLEAR AND SPACE TALKS
The Strategic Arms Reduction Talks (START) began with the 
Soviets in June 1982. They have continued in regular sessions ever 
since, broken by a 15-­month Soviet delay following our initial deploy-
ments of INF missiles. After the Soviets returned to the table in March 
1985, we conducted three negotiations in parallel—START, INF, and 
Defense & Space—­under the title of Nuclear and Space Talks (NST). 
Now that the INF Treaty is concluded, only START and Defense & 
Space remain under NST.
We have agreed with the Soviets to resume both negotiations 
June 19 in Geneva. We anticipate the first session to last about six weeks 
with a similar or longer session to follow this fall, depending on the 
status of the negotiations at that point.
