Chapter 8, June–August 1991 1101 For everything else, I have been given the following instructions: “Get it done; tell us how it came out.” This includes the authority to jettison all IP equipment annexes if the Soviets won’t agree to them. Effective now, we are out of the RFG business. For things I have to go to Washington on, I’ll E-mail something back, get a phone response from NSC, and follow-up with a cable later. Tim will put out details of how this will work. We will probably drop asterisked pages. I will check with Washington after the London meeting. Baker and Bessmyrtnykh agreed that everything is a package. The delegation should plan on remaining in Geneva for about 10 days after signature to finish MEMCONS. In return, we will not worry about timely MEMCONs until after signature. 233.
