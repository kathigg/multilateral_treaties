108 Foreign Relations, 1989–1992, Volume XXXI Tab D Talking Points Prepared in the National Security Council5 Washington, undated POINTS TO BE MADE FOR MEETING WITH THE NATIONAL SECURITY COUNCIL — Your people have done an excellent job in focusing a very broad and difficult review of the arms control implications of our defense strategy into the questions we are to address today. I’ve said it before, but need to say it again: Please convey to your staffs my thanks for the long hours and enormous effort they have put into this study. — Today will be our first of many opportunities to discuss arms control. The issues are tough, but we need to face up to them at the outset. — I want to use today to have a frank and candid discussion about the premises and policies that underpin our positions in the START and Defense and Space Talks. I want to ensure we are clear about where we are headed and that we have chosen the best road to get there. — That is the only way we can develop sound negotiating positions, achieve arms control agreements that are in our interest, and sustain the support we will need to ratify them. — Brent, let me ask you to introduce each of the issues in turn. 5 Secret.
