225.	 Telegram From the Department of State to the Embassy in 
Korea1
Washington, June 20, 1991, 1947Z
203170. Subject: Official-informal
1. Secret—Entire Text
2. I have just heard from the party in Berlin2 that Baker and 
Bessmertnykh have agreed that you and Obukhov will meet in Geneva 
beginning next Wednesday.3 That does not seem to leave us much 
choice except to leave Washington Tuesday evening, but given your 
wish for a Wednesday departure, I will not give the others the launch 
order until I hear back from you.
3. The background is as follows: I gave Dennis Ross a note before 
he left on your desire to return to Washington after your trip to China, 
Japan, and Korea and not leave before Wednesday evening. In addition 
I gave the same information to Bob Pearson over the phone yesterday.
