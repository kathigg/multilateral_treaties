According to the draft agenda (see footnote 1, above), the trip was scheduled to last from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. According to the memorandum of conversation of the trip, Baker reasserted the U.S. position that “we would sign the START treaty if the Krasnoyarsk dismantlement has started and is proceeding at a reasonable pace.” Shevardnadze responded “that I will keep my word, as stated in Wyoming.” (Department of State, Policy Planning Staff, Lot 03D102, Dennis Ross Files, US/Soviet Memos of Conversation—1989/1990). 121.
