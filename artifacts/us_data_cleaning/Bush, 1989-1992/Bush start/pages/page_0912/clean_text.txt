The obligations of paragraph 5 of Article II of the Treaty shall not affect generally recognized principles of international law relating to the passage of submarines or flights of aircraft or relating to visits of submarines to ports of States not party to this Treaty. The Parties further agree that, with regard to heavy bombers, paragraph 5 of Article II of the Treaty shall not preclude the temporary stationing for extended periods of time of heavy bombers outside the national territory of a Party. If a Party stations heavy bombers outside its national territory for a period in excess of 30 days at any one time, it shall advise the other Party through diplomatic channels before the end of the 30-­day period. Tab 3 Statement Prepared in the Department of State59 Washington, undated As we have indicated in the past the United States attaches great importance to the role played by the United Kingdom’s independent nuclear deterrent in helping to maintain world peace. As a result, the United States has engaged in a broad pattern of cooperation with the United Kingdom for many years to help maintain and modernize that deterrent. This is what we have referred to as “the existing pattern of cooperation” between the United States and the United Kingdom. It currently includes the provision by the United States to the United Kingdom of Trident SLBMs. The United States notes the statements made by British Foreign Secretary Hurd to Soviet Foreign Minister Shevardnadze regarding the future British plans for United Kingdom nuclear forces: “The British strategic force will remain a minimum one in no way comparable to the nuclear forces of the Soviet Union and the United States.” We believe this statement with regard to the United Kingdom’s independent nuclear deterrent should satisfy the Soviet Union’s concerns. 59 Secret.
