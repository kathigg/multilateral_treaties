1. Simplify the JDT. We could START with the MOU, which is full 
of extraneous material of little relevance to the treaty. Recognize 
that much of the complicated nature of the JDT comes from some 
rather questionable constraints on non-­deployed missiles. The 
review of our NDM position now being carried out should have 
simplification as a major goal.
2. Give the delegation more authority to resolve issues, ad referen-
dum to Washington, of course.
3. Try to achieve significant progress on the remaining major issues 
at every foreign minister or summit meeting. (In this regard, it 
would be useful to agree on a START agenda for such meetings so 
both sides come well prepared.)
4. Force the interagency process to fill in the remaining gaps in our 
own position on some reasonable schedule.
5. Make clear to the bureaucracy that START has a high priority for 
this administration.
Burt
