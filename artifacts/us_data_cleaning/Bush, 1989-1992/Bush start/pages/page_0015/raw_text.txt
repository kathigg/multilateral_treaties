key concepts and events. Key to this volume were the cable traffic to 
and from the U.S. Delegation to the Nuclear and Space Talks in Geneva, 
which Richard Burt led from February 17, 1989, until his deputy, Linton 
Brooks, succeeded him in March 1991. The P reels consist of micro-
filmed versions of memoranda of conversation, letters, briefing papers, 
airgrams, and memoranda to principals, among other documents. The 
National Archives and Records Administration facility in College Park, 
Maryland, will eventually include the Central Foreign Policy File as 
part of Record Group (RG) 59.
In addition to the paper files cited below, a growing number of 
documents are available on the internet. The Office of the Historian 
maintains a list of these resources on its website and encourages read-
ers to consult that site on a regular basis.
