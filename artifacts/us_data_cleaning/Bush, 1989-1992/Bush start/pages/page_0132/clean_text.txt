Mobile SS–24 deployments are already well along and will be a mainstay of Soviet survivability efforts. — Until perhaps the late 1990s, mobile SS–24s will be less accurate and thus less threatening than silo-­based SS–24s. — With a ban on mobile MX, we could not field a mobile ICBM for at least another 3–5 years. Continued uncertainty about the fate of U.S. mobiles could harm ratification. — A MIRVed mobile ban could make it politically (although not legally) impossible to put our MX back in silos later. 23. Memorandum From the President’s Assistant for National Security Affairs (Scowcroft) to President Bush1 Washington, May 25, 1989 MEETING WITH THE NATIONAL SECURITY COUNCIL I. PURPOSE For your advisors to review the arms control implications of the Defense Strategy Review (NSR 12, Part IV). A discussion paper (Tab A)2 has been prepared to assist in addressing these issues. II. BACKGROUND You have held two previous NSC meetings on the results of NSR- 12; the first was on ICBM modernization and SDI, and the second was on defense strategy. This will be the first opportunity for your NSC to discuss arms control issues.
