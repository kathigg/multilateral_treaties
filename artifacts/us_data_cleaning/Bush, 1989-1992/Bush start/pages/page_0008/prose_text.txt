VII Preface Structure and Scope of the Foreign Relations Series This volume is part of a subseries of volumes of the Foreign Relations series that documents the most important issues in the foreign policy of the administration of George H.W. Bush. Directly following Foreign Relations, 1981–1988, Volume XI, START I in the Ronald Reagan subseries, this volume is closely linked to the two volumes in the H.W. Bush subseries devoted to President Bush’s policies toward the Soviet Union, Russia, and the Newly Independent States: Foreign Relations, 1989–1992, Volume III, Soviet Union, Russia, and Post-Soviet States: High-Level Contacts and Volume IV, Soviet Union, Russia, and Post-Soviet States: Policy. It ought to be considered alongside Foreign Relations, 1989–1992, Volume XXVII, Arms Control and Nonproliferation, which includes a compilation on START II, the “deMIRVing” agreement that President Bush and Russian President Boris Yeltsin signed on January 3, 1993, and whose origins lie within late rounds of the negotiations toward START I, which President Bush and Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev signed in Moscow on July 31, 1991. When the Soviet Union ceased to exist, on December 31, 1991, the Bush administration had not yet sent START I to the U.S. Senate for ratification. Documentation on the May 23, 1992, Lisbon Protocol to START I—which incorporated Belarus, Ukraine, and Kazakhstan into the treaty and allowed the Bush administration to seek the Senate’s advice and consent—is scheduled for publication in Foreign Relations, 1993–2000, Volume XX, Arms Control and Nonproliferation within the Former Soviet Union, December 1991–December 1994.
