Chapter 5, May–June 1990  673
114.	 Memorandum From Jeffrey Starr of the Delegation to the 
Nuclear and Space Talks in Geneva to the Arms Control and 
Disarmament Agency (Lehman)1
Geneva, May 9, 1990
SUBJECT
Outstanding START Issues
As requested, attached is the list of 32 issues which the Secretary 
and the Soviet Foreign Minister agreed in April to attempt to resolve 
in Geneva before the next ministerial. You should be aware also that 
Ambassadors Burt and Nazarkin have agreed to expand the 32 issues 
list to 50 issues. The Delegation has drawn up a list of additional 
issues and will begin discussions with the Soviets tomorrow on it. 
There is no formal list of issues, internal or agreed, to be resolved 
by the time of the summit; in fact, there has been some reluctance to 
draw up such a list. Nonetheless, a list of other outstanding START 
issues is attached.
Attachment
Paper Prepared in the Delegation to the Nuclear and Space 
Talks in Geneva2
Geneva, undated
32 ISSUES
As Agreed by The Secretary and The Soviet Foreign Minister at 
the April Ministerial in Washington
1. Definition of new types of strategic offensive arms
2. Warhead attribution for future types of ballistic missiles
3. Basing terminology
4. Sublimits on mobile ICBM launchers and on warheads on mobile 
ICBMs
