Notes of a Meeting1
Washington, March 8, 1990, 3–4 p.m.
SUBJECT
Arms Control/START II
DC, JB, CP, BS, RG
DC—­events coming, prob. w. both mobile ICBMs, FOTL, time—­
where we go w. arms control. Trade in START 24 for MX, long term—­
deMIRV ICBMs.—Not fund or cancel msl. prog—­arms control strat. 
Stay where are—­lose FOTL, strat. prog. Long term objectives.
BS—Like idea. RG for SS 24—­long term START II de-MIRV ICBM 
force.
JB—Not inconsistent my view. 90 days away from Summit. 
Conclude I—Jt. declar.—P. lay out long term proposals. Reluctant to 
back off getting I initialed.
BS—All agree on that. Ann on proposal on mobiles now to be dis-
cussed at summit. Wrap up I, strategy for II.
DC—Match our legis. timetable. House—­kill both Aspin kill both.
BS—Gen. agree on substance.
CP—This deal—I agree. JCS have certified START on basis of 
approval of mod. program. esp
[4 lines not declassified]
DC—Long term can shrink size of force + bring chiefs along—­take 
a long time.
BS—Not focus on warhead #s in Start II—Improve stat. Chiefs no 
prob w. RGarr.
DC—Will reduce size of B–2 buy.
JB—­timing?
BS—­need to work out For DC purposes—­statement or jt declar 
RG–24s before Summit (jt ann. plan at summit running start or START 
II—­focus on 24/RG prelim. disc. in April).
JB—Summit not deal w outstanding Start issues.
