680  Foreign Relations, 1989–1992, Volume XXXI
117.	 Memorandum From the President’s Assistant for National 
Security Affairs (Scowcroft) to President Bush1
Washington, May 14, 1990
SUBJECT
Proposed National Security Directive (NSD) on START
Purpose
To seek your approval of a proposed National Security Directive 
providing U.S. positions on several START issues.
Background
I have reviewed the major issues that remain in START with Jim 
Baker, Dick Cheney, and Colin Powell. They concur in the proposed 
NSD (attached at Tab A) that defines the U.S. positions Jim should be 
prepared to take at the Moscow ministerial in an effort to reach closure 
with the Soviets on the basic START agreement by the Summit.
RECOMMENDATION
That you sign the proposed NSD at Tab A.2
