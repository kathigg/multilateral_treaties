Chapter 7, January–May 1991 997 Bessmertnykh: Why is the US deciding to remove throwweight from criterion, when you have decided before that you would have it? Burt: We’re afraid if throwweight is used as a criterion for new types simply because it produce endless debates, whether something is a new type or not, it’s just not a good measure for a new type. It’s good for military capability, but it’s not very good for new versus existing missiles. The alternative to exempting the missile would be for us to sort of look at existing missiles and say the throwweight increases at more than 20% would constitute a new missile.
