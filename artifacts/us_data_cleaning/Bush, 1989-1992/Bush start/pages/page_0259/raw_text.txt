POINTS TO BE MADE ON START
— We have a tough agenda for this meeting, but it is essential that 
we come to grips with the hard issues before our negotiator heads back 
to Geneva.
— Today, I would like to critically examine some of the most 
important policy issues on which we face decisions, without getting 
mired down in very technical details.
— These may be some of the most important decisions we make 
during this Administration. They could change the way we think about 
strategic forces for years to come. They will affect our deterrent capabil-
ity. They certainly will influence our support in Congress.
— Given our own plans to deploy two mobile missiles over the 
next several years, we need to examine whether to change the current 
U.S. negotiating position to ban mobile missiles.
— I also want to hear your views about how to approach the related 
issue of limits on so-­called nondeployed missiles.
— Finally, I want to review the status of air-­launched cruise missile 
limits.
— If there are significant changes to be made, this may be the best 
time. On the other hand, I am not interested in something that just 
grabs the headlines for a few days, especially if all we get is a loud 
“nyet” from Gorbachev that sets back the negotiations.
