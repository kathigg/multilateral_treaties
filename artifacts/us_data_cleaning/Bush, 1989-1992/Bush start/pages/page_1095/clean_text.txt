1068 Foreign Relations, 1989–1992, Volume XXXI Attachment Paper Prepared in the Soviet Ministry of Foreign Affairs4 Moscow, undated Pursuant to the agreement reached in Geneva we are making vigorous efforts on our part to intensify the process of completing the START Treaty. President Bush’s message has been reported to President Gorbachev, and intensive work is now under way to prepare our response. We expect that President Gorbachev’s message shall be ready by the end of the week. With this in mind, we shall be promptly notifying the Secretary of State as to when Mr. Obukhov shall be ready, on the basis of US and our own ideas, to have a discussion with R. Bartholomew and let me note that the Minister strongly supports the idea of Mr. Bartholomew’s coming to Geneva on an urgent basis. As early as tomorrow we shall be sending a group of our experts to Geneva to discuss telemetry issues. In this connection, we expect to receive shortly relevant detailed US proposals. 4 Secret. An unknown hand wrote at the top of the paper: “Oral message from A. Bessmertnykh to Secretary J. Baker.” A stamped notation indicates Scowcroft saw the paper.
