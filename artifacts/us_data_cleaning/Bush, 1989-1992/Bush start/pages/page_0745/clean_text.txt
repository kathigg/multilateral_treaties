718 Foreign Relations, 1989–1992, Volume XXXI 130. Telegram From the Department of State to the Embassy in the Soviet Union1 Washington, May 22, 1990, 0328Z 164262. Subject: Soviet Arms Control Experts Group 1. (S) Entire Text. 2. Embassy should ask MFA for the composition of the Soviet Arms Control Experts Group that is coming to Washington this week pursuant to the agreement between Foreign Minister Shevardnadze and Secretary Baker, and the timing of their arrival and departure. 3. We consider this to be a continuation of the Karpov-Bartholomew working group meetings in Moscow. We assume they will continue to address the agenda discussed in Moscow, including the following issues: — Heavy ICBMs; — Mobile ICBM limits and verification; — Backfire; — Non-­circumvention; — ICBM warhead sublimit; — Phasing of reductions 4. The group can also consider the bracketed text of the joint statement on future negotiations and PPCM, both of which are now being worked in Geneva. 5. In addition, we also look forward to Soviet responses to the CFE ideas the US put forward in Moscow. 6. As in Moscow, the purpose of this meeting would be to reach agreements on these issues to recommend to ministers. 7. You should confirm that Moscow has the same understanding of the objectives of the experts session as Washington. Baker 1 Source: Department of State, Central Foreign Policy File, [no film number].
