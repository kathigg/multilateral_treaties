The Acting Secretary of State to the Minister in Switzerland
(Wilson)
WASHINGTON, September 17, 1927—3 p.m.
80. Your despatch No. 56, August 4, 1927. Please communicate the
following to the Secretary General of the League of Nations in the usual
informal manner:
“The Secretary of State of the United States of America refers to
the note of the Deputy Secretary General of the League of
Nations, dated April 2, 1927, in which he was good enough to
invite the Government of the United States of America to attend
an international conference with a view to framing an
international convention for abolishing import and export
prohibitions and restrictions. The Government of the United
States is glad to accept the invitation of the League of Nations
and to participate in this Conference, which, it is informed will
convene at Geneva on October 17, 1927. The President has
appointed Mr. Hugh Wilson, American Minister to Switzerland to
attend the Conference as the representative of the United States.
He will be assisted by one or more advisers whose names will be
communicated to you as soon as possible.”
Full instructions will be transmitted to you later.
