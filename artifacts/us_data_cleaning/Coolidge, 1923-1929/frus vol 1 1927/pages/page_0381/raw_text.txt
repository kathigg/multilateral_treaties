Definite announcement by the British regarding their stand on the above
matters would make it possible for us to decide whether any advantage
would be gained by proceeding further with our negotiations. Therefore, we
entertain the hope that Mr. Bridgeman will, upon his return from London,
be able to give us definite replies.
In my conversation with him this evening I said that I hoped the British
would find it possible to meet us on some common ground and that he
would not return with their old proposals under different guise.
