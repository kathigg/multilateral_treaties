The chairman has now asked me whether the acceptance by the Conference
of the Rumanian reservation will prevent my Government from signing.
Such signing on our part will now imply acceptance of Rumanian
reservation. The reservation in itself appears of no economic importance to
the United States and my negative vote was intended, as I explained, only to
emphasize our attitude on the principle.
The American exception as regards helium gas was accepted without
opposition under paragraph 2, article 6 (formerly additional article).
It now becomes important to know at once Department’s attitude toward
signing. Chairman has called my attention to the fact that unless we can be
prepared at final reading Saturday night to affix our signature our
reservation on helium must be dropped for the present from the convention
and must be submitted again as one of the reservations to be included after
signature as provided in protocol [section IV, (d) ad No. 4, (ii) Procedure].24
