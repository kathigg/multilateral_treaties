Mr. R. C. Leffingwell of J. P. Morgan & Co. to the Under
Secretary of State
NEW YORK, December 14, 1927.
[Received December 15.]
DEAR MR. SECRETARY: Referring to my letter of October 18th to Secretary Kellogg
and to Assistant Secretary Castle’s reply of October 22nd,28 and to my talk
with you the other day, all about Austria, this is just a line to remind you
that discussions are going on in London between the Austrian Government
and the European Relief Creditor States, and I understand the suggestion
has been made by the Austrian Government that the Department of State
might wish to be represented.
Very truly yours,
R. C. LEFFINGWELL
28 Not printed.
