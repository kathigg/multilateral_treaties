124.42/9 [Document 349] The Chargé in Great Britain (Sterling) to the Secretary of State LONDON, January 10, 1927. [Received January 22.] No. 1569 SIR: Referring to the Embassy’s telegram No. 6, dated January 7th, 11 a.m., 1927,2 I have the honor to enclose herewith a copy, in triplicate, of the Note referred to therein from the Foreign Office, with regard to the appointments of Ministers from the United States to Canada and the Irish Free State. I have [etc.] F. A. STERLING [Enclosure] The British Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs (Chamberlain) to the American Chargé (Sterling) LONDON, 6 January, 1927. No. T 152/92/373 SIR: Mr. Houghton3 was good enough to inform Sir William Tyrrell4 last month, on instructions from the United States Government, of the intention of the President of the United States of America to appoint United States Ministers at Ottawa and Dublin in view of the appointment of His Majesty’s Ministers at Washington to represent the interests of Canada and the Irish Free State, and His Excellency enquired whether these appointments would be agreeable.
