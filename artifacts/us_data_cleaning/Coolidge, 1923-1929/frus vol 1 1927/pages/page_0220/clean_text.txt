The French Government, appreciative of the value that the American Government attaches to its being directly informed concerning the conversations engaged in between the three powers, preserves the greatest sympathy for the American efforts for disarmament and for peace. It would certainly have liked to be able to decide now as to the cordial invitation which has been sent it. It likes at least to think that the American Government will appreciate the reasons which make it a duty under the present circumstances to defer any decision as to the possible participation, even by a simple observer, in conversations on a limited subject touching on the question of disarmament.” HERRICK 24 Telegram in two sections. 25 See telegram No. 72, Mar. 12, 4 p.m., to the Ambassador in France, p. 28 [Pg. 28 includes portions of Doc. 25 and Doc. 26].
