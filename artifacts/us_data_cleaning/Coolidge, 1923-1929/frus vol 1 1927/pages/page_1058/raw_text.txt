The British Ambassador (Howard) to the Secretary of State
WASHINGTON, January 24, 1927.
No. 49
SIR: I have the honour to refer to your note of December 4th last,6 notifying
me that the appointment of the Honourable Vincent Massey as His
Majesty’s Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary to represent
the interests of the Dominion of Canada in the United States will be entirely
agreeable to the United States Government.
In this connection, I have the honour to state that Mr. Massey has now
advised me that he proposes to arrive at Washington at the end of the
second week in February and is desirous of presenting his letters of
credence on any day between the 15th and 19th of February. I should
accordingly be most grateful if you would be so good as to notify me at
your earliest convenience on what date it will be convenient for the
President to receive Mr. Massey for the presentation of his credentials. At
the same time, I have the honour to inform you that the Government of
Canada are desirous that I should accompany Mr. Massey to the White
House on this occasion.
I have [etc.]
ESME HOWARD
6 Foreign Relations, 1926, vol.  I, p. 580.
