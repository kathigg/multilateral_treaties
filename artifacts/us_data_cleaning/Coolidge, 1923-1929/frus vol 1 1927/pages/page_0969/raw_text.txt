The Commissioner for Australia (Denison) to the Secretary of
State
NEW YORK, 14 January, 1927.
[Received January 15.]
DEAR MR. SECRETARY OF STATE: In connection with the recent visit of the Right Hon. S.
M. Bruce, Prime Minister of Australia, to Washington, when he had the
pleasure of an interview with you, the question of the position of Australian
business men visiting the United States of America was discussed in the
presence of His Excellency the British Ambassador, and myself, who
accompanied Mr. Bruce. At the close of that interview you suggested that I
should furnish you with a report on the existing position for your further
consideration. I now have the honour of submitting same herewith.
It should be noted that the entrance of Australian citizens is controlled by
certain regulations of the Bureau of Immigration, United States Department
of Labor. These regulations may be defined broadly as:—
(a) Entrance under the quota allotted to the Commonwealth of
Australia.
(b) Entrance as transients,
(c) Entrance of persons who visit the United States as students.
(d) Entrance as persons engaged in commerce, and who are
granted liberty to remain in the country for a limited period
(generally from three to six months), with the privilege of
applying to the Bureau of Immigration for an extension of time.
