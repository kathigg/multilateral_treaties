500.A15/483: Telegram
[Document 177]
The Secretary of State to the Chief of the American
Representation on the Preparatory Commission (Gibson)
[Paraphrase]
WASHINGTON, April 11, 1927—2 p.m.
114. Your No. 227, April 10, 6 p.m. The Department, as might be expected,
disagrees entirely with Boncour’s position that to continue discussion of
other phases of draft convention would be futile without an agreement on
naval matters. If, however, an adjournment is desired, the Department
perceives no objection.
No conflict with the Three-Power Naval Conference is to be feared, in any
event, as the Department has maintained the feasibility of carrying on these
conversations during the work of the Preparatory Commission.
KELLOGG
