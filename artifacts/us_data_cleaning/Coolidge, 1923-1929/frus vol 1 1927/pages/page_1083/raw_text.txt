General Order No. 86 of the Department of Labor
WASHINGTON, April 1, 1927.
SUBJECT: Land border crossing procedure.
1. Hereafter aliens residing in foreign contiguous countries and entering the
United States to engage in existing employment or to seek employment in
this country will not be considered as visiting the United States temporarily
as tourists, or temporarily for business or pleasure, under any provisions of
the Immigration Law which exempt visitors from complying with certain
requirements thereof; that is, they will be considered as aliens of the
“immigrant” class.
