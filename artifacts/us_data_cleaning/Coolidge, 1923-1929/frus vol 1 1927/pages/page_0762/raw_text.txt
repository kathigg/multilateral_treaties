Colombia and Nicaragua to conclude a treaty by which Nicaraguan
sovereignty is recognized over the Mosquito Coast and Great and Little
Corn Island[s] and Colombia would also in the same act confirm the rights
of the United States to Great and Little Corn Islands obtained by the Treaty
with Nicaragua of 1914.60 Nicaragua would recognize Colombian
sovereignty over the Islands of San Andrés and Providencia, and Colombia
and the United States would submit to arbitration the ownership of the other
keys and Colombia would agree, should she win the arbitration, to sell these
keys to the United States, should the United States so desire, for a
consideration to be agreed upon between the two Governments.
The Minister stated that it was a question of amour propre for Colombia as
she could not well give up the Islands or recognize American jurisdiction
over them except through arbitration and that he felt sure that Colombia
would accept anybody proposed by the United States as arbitrator. I told the
Minister I would look into the matter and advise him later.
