721.2315/326: Telegram
[Document 261]
The Ambassador in Peru (Poindexter) to the Secretary of State
[Paraphrase]
LIMA, January 12, 1927—6 p.m. 
[Received 9:50 p.m.]
4. President Leguía told the Colombian Minister that, due to the delicate
situation occasioned by the Tacna-Arica question, he would be unable to
submit the boundary treaty to the present Congress. The Colombian
Minister replied that, because of reiterated promises made by the President
and the Foreign Office on various specific occasions during the last few
years and especially during the last few months that the treaty would be
submitted to Congress and promptly ratified and the fact that they had
failed to fulfill any of these promises, he considered it useless to negotiate
further on this subject with the Government of Peru and he would not return
again to discuss the subject with him.
. . . . . . . . . . . . . .
The Colombian Minister also told President Leguía that if the treaty were
not submitted and approved by this Congress, a dangerous condition of
public opinion in Colombia would be created. President Leguía seemed
greatly embarrassed and assured the Colombian Minister that the present
state of affairs was due to circumstances beyond his control. He begged him
to be patient, and again assured him that his intention to have the treaty
ratified at some future date was as strong as ever. The Colombian Minister
appears to have little confidence in this and has informed his Government
of the present state of affairs.
POINDEXTER
