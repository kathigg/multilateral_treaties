825.506/16: Telegram
[Document 410]
The Ambassador in Chile (Collier) to the Secretary of State
SANTIAGO, September 21, 1927—3 p.m. 
[Received 7:40 p.m.]
138. Regular session of Congress has terminated. Government contemplates
calling special session about October 6th. Insurance bill will then come
before Senate. I request Department’s consideration of the fact that the tax
on policies negotiated by foreigners with foreign companies and executed
in foreign countries affects not only insurance on real estate, that is,
buildings, but also insurance on personal property, machinery and other
chattels and also insurance against loss of prospective profits caused by
interruption of business due to fire. Am informed that one of three great
American copper companies carries 12 million dollars against loss of
prospective profits, presumably the others carry proportionate insurance.
Attorneys for American interests have argued that if such contracts relating
to personal property even though used in connection with or derived from
real estate situated in Chile can thus be taxed, it would appear that any
contract affecting such personal property, as for instance mortgages and
assignments, could be taxed. Does this in the Department’s opinion affect
its decision that no representation should be made against this feature of the
law?
COLLIER
