In view of the action already taken by both governments, it is apprehended that they are in accord on the principle that the project should be undertaken. If this Government’s conclusion in this respect be correct, there only remains to be effected an understanding as to the methods and means for its earliest accomplishment. It seems highly appropriate that the development of the common highway for the benefit of both countries should be jointly undertaken. This Government is prepared to enter into negotiations with a view to the formulation of a convention appropriate to this subject and should be grateful to be informed of the views entertained on this subject by your Government. Accept [etc.] FRANK B. KELLOGG
