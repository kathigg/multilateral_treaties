500.A15 a 1/457: Telegram
[Document 116]
The Secretary of State to the Chairman of the American
Delegation (Gibson)
[Paraphrase]
WASHINGTON, July 20, 1927—3 p.m.
57. Your No. 110, July 19, midnight. After my telegram to Houghton last
week,73 it is somewhat embarrassing to take matter up again with British
Government. I am willing, however, to do anything that will be of
assistance in bringing about an agreement.
In regard to your suggestions, the first seems to be taken care of by my
telegram to Houghton; do not fully understand the second.
If I go no further than to say to the British that we cannot accept limitation
of unit displacement of cruisers which would preclude armament of
efficient battery of 8-inch guns, would not British assume that we are
willing to make agreement to build cruisers below 10,000 tons with 8-inch
guns?
KELLOGG
73 Quoted in telegram No. 50, July 16, 1 p.m., to the chairman of the
American delegation, p. 108 [Pg. 108 includes portions of Doc. 104, Doc. 105, and Doc.
106].
