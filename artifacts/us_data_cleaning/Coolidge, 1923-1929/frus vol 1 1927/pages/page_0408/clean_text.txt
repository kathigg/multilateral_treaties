500.A15 a 1/494: Telegram [Document 132] The Chairman of the American Delegation (Gibson) to the Secretary of State [Paraphrase] GENEVA, July 28, 1927—2 p.m. [Received July 28—10:05 a.m.] 122. This morning Bridgeman called and, although he did not reveal full details of the new British proposals, he did not indicate that any substantial change had been made in the previous British insistence on small cruisers and armament of 6-inch guns. The delegates of the three powers are having a meeting this afternoon. We consider it extremely unlikely, in view of the foregoing, that a breakdown can be avoided, and this afternoon shall telegraph a general outline of our proposed statement for the final plenary session. We shall insist that sufficient time be allowed us before plenary session in order to communicate fully with you and to receive your final instructions. GIBSON
