500.A16/51: Telegram
[Document 254]
The American Representative on the Special Commission
(Wilson) to the Secretary of State
GENEVA, August 27, 1928—noon.
[Received August 27—9:12 a.m.]
1. Article V of your draft of treaty discussing category III appears to limit
“civil aircraft” to those “manufactured under military specifications.”
Examination of Traffic in Arms Convention discloses no such limitation
and I would appreciate analysis of Department’s views in this matter. I have
requested Yount59 to proceed from Paris to advise me on this and other
questions.
I assume that full publicity concerning aircraft is made by our Government
departments. If this is so could we not safely promise full publicity on “civil
aviation” without limitation?
Address Ammission.
WILSON
59 Major Burton K. Yount, assistant military attaché for air, American
Embassy, Paris.
