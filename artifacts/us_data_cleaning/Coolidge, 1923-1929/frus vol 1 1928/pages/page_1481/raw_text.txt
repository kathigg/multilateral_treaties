The Secretary of State to the Ambassador in Peru (Poindexter)
WASHINGTON, February 6, 1928.
No. 412
SIR: The Department has received and read with interest your despatch, No.
883 of January 3 last,44 reporting upon the interest shown by President
Leguía in a friendly settlement of the existing boundary difficulties between
Bolivia and Paraguay. It appears from this despatch that President Leguía
has suggested to the Bolivian and Paraguayan Governments that they
should seek the assistance of the United States in bringing the dispute to a
peaceable conclusion.
[Here follow two paragraphs in the sense of the Department’s instruction
No. 333, February 4, 1928, to the Chargé in Bolivia, printed supra.]
I am [etc.]
FRANK B. KELLOGG
44 Not printed.
