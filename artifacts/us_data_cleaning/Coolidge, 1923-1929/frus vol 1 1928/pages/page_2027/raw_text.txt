863.51 Relief Credits/102
[Document 760]
Memorandum by the Economic Adviser (Young)
[WASHINGTON,] April 3, 1928.
The Austrian Minister stated that he had been instructed to inform the
Department in confidence that at the meeting of the relief creditors last
week all the Governments, excepting Italy, agreed to accept the Austrian
proposal for settlement of relief debts. The attitude of Italy, he stated, is
undoubtedly caused by the Tyrol incident.
The Minister stated that as long as the United States had not adopted the
legislation authorizing it to act in relation to the lien subordination and debt
settlement, the Italian Government could defer action and say that the
United States had not yet acted. If, however, the United States should take
action, Italy would be isolated. The Minister anticipated that the British
Government and possibly others would bring pressure to bear on Italy not
to block the whole enterprise.
The Minister also stated that he would be very glad to go forward with
actual negotiations concerning the relief debt in order that the agreement
might be put in shape.
I told the Minister that no date had yet been set, so far as I knew, for
hearings on the proposed legislation. This Department and the Treasury,
however, would do whatever they could to keep the proposal actively
before Congress. I stated that I would discuss the matter with the
Treasury.47
A[RTHUR] N. Y[OUNG]
