The memoranda submitted with the British Ambassador’s note of December 16, 1921, covered two principal subjects, namely, (1) enemy-owned shares in American companies claimed by the Public Trustee and by the Alien Property Custodian, and (2) claims by nationals of the respective Governments for the release of property seized by the other as enemy property. The memorandum with respect to shares of stock contained three paragraphs reading as follows: “1. The Alien Property Custodian on behalf of the United States Government concedes that all bearer securities in the hands of the Custodian and being the obligations of Companies incorporated in the United States shall be the property of the Custodian for England and Wales. “2.
