711.6212Anti-War/64 [Document 85] The Ambassador in Germany (Schurman) to the Secretary of State BERLIN, July 11, 1928. [Received July 23.] No. 3706 SIR: Confirming my telegram No. 149 of July 11, 1928,91 I have the honor to transmit herewith, in copy and translation, text of the reply of the German Government, received under even date, to the note of June 23, 1928, transmitting the revised draft of the proposed antiwar treaty. I have [etc.] JACOB GOULD SCHURMAN [Enclosure—Translation] The State Secretary of the German Foreign Office (Schubert) to the American Ambassador (Schurman) BERLIN, July 11, 1928. V M 3323 MR. AMBASSADOR: I acknowledge the receipt of Your Excellency’s note of June 23, 1928, regarding the conclusion of an international pact for the renunciation of war, and have the honor to reply thereto as follows on behalf of the German Government. The German Government has examined with the greatest care the contents of the note and the revised draft of the pact which was enclosed.
