You should, therefore, explain to Chamberlain that I never suggested a conference of Foreign Ministers for the purpose of negotiating and agreeing upon a form of treaty, and that in my opinion such a conference is wholly unnecessary. The position of the United States is clearly set forth in its notes and in the draft treaty it has proposed. The position of Germany is no less clearly indicated in its unqualified acceptance of the American proposal. If the United States and Germany can determine their respective positions on this important subject without an international conference, I can see no reason why the British, French, Italian and Japanese Governments cannot do likewise.
