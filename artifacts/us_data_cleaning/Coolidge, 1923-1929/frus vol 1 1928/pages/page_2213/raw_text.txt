“The Conference,
“considering that it is advisable to facilitate legal assistance for
emigrants and their families,
“expresses the Wish:
“(a) that the States which have not yet concluded
conventions on this subject, and particularly emigration
and immigration States, should establish, by means of
agreements based on reciprocity, equality of treatment
with nationals as regards access to judicial assistance;
“(b) that when consular conventions are being
negotiated, the States concerned should consider the
possibility of authorising consuls, in the interests of
their nationals who may be absent, to approach the
administrative and judicial authorities, in order that
their nationals may avoid prescription or the forfeiture
of the rights which they possess under the laws for
social insurance. The steps taken by the consuls must be
confirmed by the parties concerned in the forms
prescribed by the laws of the country of residence;
“(c) that each Government should encourage the
formation and facilitate the work of private associations
for legal assistance to emigrants.”
Reference is also made to Article XVII of the Convention for Civil
Procedure of July 17, 1905,16 which provides as follows:
“Aucune caution ni dépôt, sous quelque dénomination que ce soit,
ne peut être imposé, à raison soit de leur qualité d’étrangers, soit
du défaut de domicile ou de résidence dans le pays, aux nationaux
d’un des États Contractants, ayant leur domicile dans l’un de ces
États, qui seront demandeurs ou intervenants devant les tribunaux
d’un autre de ces Étas.
