Please accept [etc.] CLAUDEL [Enclosure—Translation] The French Ambassador (Claudel) to the Secretary of State WASHINGTON, January 5, 1928. MR. SECRETARY OF STATE: By a letter of December 28th last your Excellency was kind enough to make known the sentiments of the Government of the United States concerning the suggestion of a treaty proposed by the Government of the Republic in the month of June 1927,3 with a view to the condemnation of war and the renunciation thereof as an instrument of national policy between France and the United States. According to your Excellency, the two governments, instead of limiting themselves to a bilateral treaty, would contribute more fully to the peace of the world by uniting their efforts to obtain the adhesion of all the principal powers of the world to a declaration renouncing war as an instrument of their national policy.
