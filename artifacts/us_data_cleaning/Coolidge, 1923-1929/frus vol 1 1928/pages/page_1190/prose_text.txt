FIFTH COMMISSION Examination of the resolutions of the Rome Conference and sequence to be given them. Mr. Carter and Mr. Nichols. In view of the nature of their instructions, the American delegates took a passive part in the debates of the Conference, confining themselves to declarations of American policy and discussion of technical methods employed by the United States, and abstained from voting, both in the commission meetings and in the plenary sessions.
