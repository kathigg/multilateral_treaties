Feb. 14 (2632) To the Ambassador in France Information that on February 2 the French Ambassador stated that Briand’s reply to the Secretary of State’s proposition must not be construed as a definite refusal to make the treaty but as a suggestion that other French obligations be taken into consideration. 8 [Pg. includes portions of Doc. 4 and Doc. 5] Feb. 27 To the French Ambassador Inability to understand how unequivocal and unqualified renunciation of war as an instrument of national policy could violate obligations imposed by the Covenant of the League of Nations or conflict with idea and purpose of the League; renewal of suggestion that Briand’s original proposal and correspondence between the two Governments be laid before British, German, Italian, and Japanese Governments.
