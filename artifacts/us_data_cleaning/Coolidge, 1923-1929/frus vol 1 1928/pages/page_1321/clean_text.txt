ARTICLE 21 The employee who substitutes for the consular agent in his absence, or for another cause, shall enjoy during his temporary term of office the same immunities and prerogatives as the latter. ARTICLE 22 Consuls engaged in business or exercising other functions apart from those pertaining to their consular duties are subject to local jurisdiction in all their activities not pertaining to the consular service. SECTION III—Suspension and termination of consular functions ARTICLE 23 Consular agents suspend their functions because of illness or leave of absence, and terminate their office: a) By death; b) By retirement, resignation, or dismissal; and c) By the cancellation of the exequatur. ARTICLE 24 The present convention does not affect obligations previously undertaken by the contracting parties through international agreements. ARTICLE 25 After being signed, the present convention shall be submitted to the ratification of the signatory states.
