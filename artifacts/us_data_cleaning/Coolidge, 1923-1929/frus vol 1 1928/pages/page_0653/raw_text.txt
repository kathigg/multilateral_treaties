The Hungarian Minister for Foreign Affairs (Walko) to the American Minister (Wright)
BUDAPEST, October 6, 1928.
3993 pol. 1928
MONSIEUR LE MINISTRE: In reply to your Excellency’s kind note Nr. 88 of August 27,
a. c. and in connection with my note of September 1, I have the honor to
inform Your Excellency of the following:
The Government of the United States is aware of the fact, that after the
World War Hungary was forced to sign a peace treaty which had not been
preceded by the negotiations customary on such occasion and which, being
the issue of erroneous presuppositions, created an unjust situation.
The situation arisen in consequence of this treaty of peace has not been able
to assure the tranquillity, and is impeding the development, of the different
nations in this part of Europe. The events of the ten years which have
elapsed since the end of the War furnish evident proof that this peace treaty
cannot be a suitable basis for natural and peaceful development.
