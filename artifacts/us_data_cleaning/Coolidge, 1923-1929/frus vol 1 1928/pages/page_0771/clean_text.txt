A copy of any remarks Willert gives out here on this subject will be telegraphed to the British Embassy in Washington. [Paraphrase.] Both Marriner and I feel that Cushendun is attempting to remove any atmosphere of suspicion touching this whole subject and is not in any way endeavoring to obtain any international recognition for British Foreign Office. From manner in which he intends to handle this matter we have concluded that he was looking forward to opportunity of renewing his acquaintance with you when you are in Paris for signature of the renunciation treaty, since he remembered you so vividly from your days in London, and that he hoped you would not feel under any pressure to answer questions raised by the Anglo-French conversations before your departure. Cushendun’s reception of us confirmed a statement which a member of the Government made to me that with his taking office any American question would meet with understanding attention. Copy sent to Brussels. ATHERTON 42 J.
