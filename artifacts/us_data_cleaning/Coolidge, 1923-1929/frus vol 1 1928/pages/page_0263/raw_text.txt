That war of aggression constitutes an international crime against

It resolves:
1. All aggression is considered illicit and as such is

2. The American States will employ all pacific means to

[Paraphrase]
Both of the said resolutions, it appears, were passed at final plenary session
Havana Conference. The Conference obviously intended to go on record as
opposed to all war; having condemned war as an instrument of national
policy, the Conference found no difficulty in condemning aggressive war as
well. It is not necessary, perhaps, to point out the difference between
resolutions like these adopted at an international conference and formal
treaties which are entered into with idea of preventing recourse to war as far
as it is possible so to do. The objection we have to concluding a treaty
limited by incorporation of an attempted definition of aggression is that,
first, it seems to us to be difficult, if not impossible, to obtain working
definition of aggressive war; and second, that even if it were theoretically
possible to obtain such a definition, the result in practice would be to defeat
largely if not entirely the main object that all of us are seeking.
Repeat to Embassies at London, Berlin and Rome.
