The Secretary of State to the Chargé in Great Britain (Atherton)
WASHINGTON, August 2, 1928—6 p.m.
180. I received yesterday from the British Embassy a communication from
Sir Austen Chamberlain embodying the understanding reached between
Great Britain and France in relation to naval limitation, with the added
suggestion that it should be adopted as a basis for discussion by the
Preparatory Conference. I have asked the Embassy for the explanation of
certain points which I do not fully understand as follows:
[Here follows substance of the note of August 2, 1928, to the British
Chargé, printed on page 266 [Pg. 266 is part of Doc. 231].]
[Paraphrase.] I think it might be well for you to see Chamberlain and
discuss with him the various points covered by my inquiry. He will
undoubtedly provide you with a copy of the agreement, should you not
already have seen it, and I think you might be able through conversation to
have more light thrown on the subject than I shall get in writing from the
Embassy here. [End paraphrase.]
KELLOGG
