If I understand thoroughly the Department’s point of view as to this
reservation, it is based more on the implications involved than on objections
to the publication of the information that would be demanded by the
Convention. I understand that our objection is to the inclusion of all
aviation as “implements of war” and the resulting possibility of so branding
civil aviation. It had seemed to me that there was a possibility that the note
drawn up by the French delegate, or one of revised phraseology, might
expressly disclaim any prejudging of the question as to “potentiel de
guerre”, and at the same time furnish a basis on which we could all agree to
this category. I therefore stated that I had no idea how my Government
would view this proposal, but I would transmit it to Washington. When it
became apparent that no possible result could be obtained from the meeting,
I stated that I maintained our reserve as it was not worth while in the
present circumstances further to examine this proposal.
