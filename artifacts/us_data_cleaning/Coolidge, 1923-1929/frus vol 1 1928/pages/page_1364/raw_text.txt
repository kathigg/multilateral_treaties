The Chargé in Uruguay (Gade) to the Secretary of State
MONTEVIDEO, May 10, 1928.
[Received June 8.]
No. 620
SIR: In compliance with the Department’s instruction No. 129 of April 14,
1928,77 I hava the honor to report that I today presented to the Minister for
Foreign Affairs a note concerning the proposed Conference of Arbitration
and Conciliation to be held at Washington on December 10, 1928.
In a conversation with the Chief of Protocol (Director de Secciones e
Introductor de Embajadores) before my interview with the Minister for
Foreign Affairs, Sr. Yeregui volunteered the information that the
Department’s proposals would surely receive a hearty acceptance as
Uruguay was unreservedly in favor of arbitration. He further declared that
Uruguay had up to now refused to conclude arbitration treaties which
contained reservations, even on matters of sovereignty.
The Minister for Foreign Affairs, Sr. Dominguez, upon receiving the note
stated that Uruguay was in favor of the principle of arbitration and that an
early answer might be expected.
I have [etc.]
GERHARD GADE
77 See footnote 66, p. 621 [Pg. 621 includes portions of Doc. 369 and Doc. 370].
