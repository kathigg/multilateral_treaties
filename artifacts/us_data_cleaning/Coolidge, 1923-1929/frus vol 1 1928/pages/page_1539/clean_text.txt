The Council believes it to be true that in two directions, with high authority, efforts are now being put forth with a view to avoiding war and to solving by specific means existing difficulties. It is in this sense that the Argentine Government and the Pan American Arbitration Conference, now meeting at Washington under the Presidency of the Secretary of State of the United States, are acting. However, the Council has not received any official information from either. The Argentine Government and the Governments represented at the Pan-American Conference are at the present moment completely informed as to the steps taken by the Council and the answers of the Governments of the two countries. In the interests of peace it seems essential in the eyes of the Council to coordinate perfectly the efforts of all those who are endeavoring to obtain a settlement of the controversy by specific [pacific] means.
