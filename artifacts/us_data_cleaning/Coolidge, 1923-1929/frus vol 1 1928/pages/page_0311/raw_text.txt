The Ambassador in Germany (Schurman) to the Secretary of
State
BERLIN, May 1, 1928.
[Received May 14.]
No. 3473
SIR: With reference to the Department’s telegram No. 43, (Paris No. 118) of
April 23, 5 p.m., on the question of a multilateral treaty for the renunciation
of war, I have the honor to transmit herewith an Aide Memoire45 thereon
which I handed to the Minister for Foreign Affairs, Dr. Stresemann on April
27.
In addition to the foregoing and in confirmation of the Embassy’s telegram
No. 85 of April 28, 11 a.m.,45 I have the honor to transmit, in the original as
well as translation, the reply of the German Government, dated April 27, to
the note which I handed Dr. Stresemann on April 13 on the same subject
(See Embassy’s telegram No. 72 of April 13, 7 p.m.46 )
I have [etc.]
JACOB GOULD SCHURMAN
