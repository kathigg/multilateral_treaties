The Alien Property Custodian (Sutherland) to the Secretary of
State20
WASHINGTON, May 23, 1928.
SIR: I have the honor to reply to your letter of May 21,21 in which you
enclosed a copy of a communication from the Minister of Austria.22
The Minister desires to know what will become of property seized as
belonging to Austrian citizens if no claim is filed within a year or if proof is
not submitted as to ownership, as provided in the Settlement of War Claims
Act of 1928, and he asks whether or not it would be credited to the Austrian
Government under Section 7, of the said Act.
You are advised that it is the view of this office that there is no law
authorizing the transfer of said sums. Subsection (g) of Section 2523 is
specific as to what sums should be transferred to this account and it
mentions none other than Austrian property or that of a corporation in
which all of the stock is owned by the Austrian Government.
Respectfully,
HOWARD SUTHERLAND
