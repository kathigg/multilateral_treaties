yes or something like that.
I told Sir Esme that I would see him before he went abroad and if I had
anything concrete that I could give him I would do so, but that I understood
that Sir Austen would be out of the country. Sir Esme was very much
surprised and I read him that part of Mr. Houghton’s telegram which states
that Sir Austen was leaving on the 26th of May for Geneva and would be
absent until the last part of June, and that he planned to leave on his
vacation the first week in September. The Ambassador was very much
surprised and said that he did not know of any business which Sir Austen
had in Geneva now.
