The Minister in Honduras (Summerlin) to the Secretary of State
TEGUCIGALPA, May 24, 1928.
[Received June 5.]
No. 616
SIR: With further reference to the Department’s instruction No. 250 of April
14, 192888 and to my despatch No. 594 of May 5, 1928, relative to a
conference of Pan American states planned to be held at Washington in
December next for the purpose of drawing up a convention of conciliation
and arbitration, I have the honor to report that the Minister for Foreign
Affairs has informed me his Government is in favor of the arbitration of
international differences. He stated further that this has been for many years
the traditional policy of his country.
I have [etc.]
GEORGE T. SUMMERLIN
88 See footnote 72, p. 623 [Pg. 623 includes portions of Doc. 371 and Doc. 372].
