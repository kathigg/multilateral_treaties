The Chargé in Peru (Hanna) to the Secretary of State
[Paraphrase]
LIMA, October 3, 1928—7 p.m. 
[Received 10:15 p.m.]
115. Your No. 77, October 2, noon. President Leguia, whom I have just
seen, says that I may inform you that he accepts your suggestion in the
same spirit that he accepted your earlier suggestion to renew diplomatic
relations with the Government of Chile. President Leguia gave me this
answer without hesitation, adding that the suspension of the proceedings of
the Boundary Commission will be regarded as a diplomatic victory by the
Government of Chile in its efforts to maintain its claim to territory never
embraced in the Treaty of Ancon.36
HANNA
36 i. e., treaty of peace of Oct. 20, 1883, Foreign Relations , 1883, p. 731.
