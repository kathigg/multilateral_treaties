724.3415/246: Telegram
[Document 453]
The Minister in Paraguay (Kreeck) to the Secretary of State
ASUNCIÓN, December 9, 1928—9 p.m. 
[Received December 11—12:22 a.m.]
28. By the breaking of diplomatic relations Bolivia refuses to accept the
Gondra Treaty as set forth by Paraguay. The Paraguayan Government has
the opinion that Bolivia must accept the provisions of the Gondra Treaty to
which both countries are signatory parties. In the interest of peace and the
purpose of the Kellogg Pact, it is believed that the American Government
should counsel Bolivia to accept the provisions established in that treaty.
KREECK
