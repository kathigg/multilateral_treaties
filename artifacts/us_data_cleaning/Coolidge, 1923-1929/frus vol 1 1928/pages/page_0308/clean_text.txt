I learn that Grey of Falloden, in addressing a large meeting of Laborites and Liberals a couple of days ago in a committee room of Parliament, made statement that he thought treaty should be signed with no reservations, but that each signatory, if it so desired, should make a supplementary statement covering its interpretation of any phrase or portion of the text. I have heard rumor that because of ill-health Briand may resign within the next fortnight and that Poincaré will take over the Foreign Office.
