The Secretary of State to the Chargé in China (Mayer)
WASHINGTON, July 13, 1925—4 p.m.
146. Your 268, July 10, 6 p.m. You are authorized, if need be in consultation with the
Chinese authorities, to reconsider the status of the administration of the International
Settlement at Shanghai as now constituted under existing regulations with a view to the
definite establishment of the Settlement upon a basis eliminating any possibility of
doubt that the diplomatic representatives have an authority commensurate with their
international responsibilities. London informed.
KELLOGG
