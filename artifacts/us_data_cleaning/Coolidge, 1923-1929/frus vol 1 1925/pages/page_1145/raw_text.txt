[Document 450]
The Minister in China (MacMurray) to the Secretary of State
[Extract]
PEKING, November 28, 1925—6 p.m.
[Received 7:49 p.m.]
502. 1. Report in first paragraph my 497 November 25, 5 p.m. partially erroneous.
Latest information is that while Yang Yu-ting has fled to Dairen [and] serious defection
among Fengtien forces south of the Wall amounted to factional fighting, there has been
no coup d’état at Mukden and Chang Tso-lin is still in control there. Although the
situation is still undefined it would seem that General Kuo Sung-ling and the
Manchurian forces between Tientsin and the Wall, having come to an agreement with
Marshal Feng Yu-hsiang, attempted to march on Mukden with the purpose of ousting
Marshal Chang, which attempt was blocked by loyal Manchurian forces at
Shanhaikwan.
. . . . . . . . . . . . . .
MACMURRAY
