Aug. 14
From the Consul at Arica (tel.)
From Pershing: Information that the restrictions referred to are
embodied in two decrees regulating travel; that the Ordonez
incident was regrettable but not important; that there is no doubt
of Chilean oppression and intimidation of Peruvians and that
under existing conditions a fair plebiscite is impossible.

Aug. 14
To the Consul at Arica (tel.)
For Pershing: Opinion that the two decrees regulating travel
violate the decision of the Arbitrator. View that Plebiscitary
Commission has power to decide whether any act of Chile’s
administration of Tacna and Arica would obstruct fair and free
vote, and inquiry whether Pershing wishes Secretary to take any
action before matter is decided by Commission.
