Although the Chargé has doubtless reported this conversation, you may take occasion
to assure yourself that situation is fully understood by Japanese Foreign Office. It is not
necessary for me to assure you that I am aware of danger of appearing to work with
other powers in disregard of Japan. This has neither been done nor intended. Press
publicity has tended to give great definiteness to what is entirely nebulous at this time
and has been the more embarrassing to me because of difficulty of making any
effectual denial without running the danger of chilling public interest and sympathy for
the further plans of naval limitation which it is still our wish to bring about.
HUGHES
