The declarations of the President of the United States during the war on the subject of the complete restoration of Belgium, the constitution [sic] developed in the course of the peace negotiations, and which demonstrated the necessity of according to my country a special treatment in satisfaction of its recognized right to such restoration, constitute a right which continues to exist whatever may have been the fate of article 232. During the progress of the conversations which are about to commence the two Governments should take this into account.
