The Government of the United States does not demand for itself treatment more favorable than that which has been accorded by Rumania to its other creditors on account of relief and reconstruction loans, but the loans made by the Government of the United States are certainly no less entitled to repayment than the loans made by the other Governments in question for similar purposes and the Government of the United States feels constrained to repeat that it is unable to agree that Rumania should make no payments on account of its relief and reconstruction indebtedness to it, while making payments to other Governments on account of an indebtedness toward them incurred for similar purposes. The principle at issue, in the opinion of the Government of the United States, is one susceptible of satisfactory settlement without the delay and expense incident to the visit to Washington of a Rumanian Debt Mission. Furthermore, the Government of the United States feels that no useful purpose could be served by the visit of such a mission if, as indicated in the Rumanian Government’s note of [March 30] it should be authorized merely to offer an explanation of the failure of the Rumanian Government to present a debt funding proposal.
