His Majesty’s Government would therefore be very glad to know how the United States
Government consider that this serious situation can best be met, how they would view a
possible proposal to place China on the list of prohibited areas and, failing this,
whether they can suggest any other means of effective international cooperation to cope
with this evil which experience has shown the Arms Embargo Agreement is insufficient
to check. His Majesty’s Government have themselves no intention of bringing forward
this proposal but they are earnestly desirous of cooperating with the United States
Government in any way possible in order to find an exit from the present impasse.
 
Sir Esme Howard would be grateful to learn the views of the United States
Government on this subject as soon as possible in order to communicate them to Mr.
Chamberlain without delay.
[ WASHINGTON ,] March 16, 1925.
