893.5045/63: Telegram
[Document 496]
The Consul General at Shanghai (Cunningham) to the Secretary of State
SHANGHAI, June 4, 1925—6 p.m.
[Received June 4—8:33 a.m.]
New developments today include strike of dining-room boys, Astor House and some
other hotels, Shanghai club and domestic servants. Strike seems to be extending and is
certainly more effective.
Chinese Chamber of Commerce is now approaching foreign chambers of commerce to
bring about better conditions.
Great Eastern and Burlington Hotels were searched this morning and considerable
Bolshevik literature and munitions was [were] found in certain rooms. All public
wharves expecting [excepting?] Dollar and Jardine Matheson are closed on account of
strike. Chinese banks have been closed for 2 days.
CUNNINGHAM
