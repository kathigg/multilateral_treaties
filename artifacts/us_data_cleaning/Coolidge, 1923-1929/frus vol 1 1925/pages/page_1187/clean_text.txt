So long as Soviet Russia claims to be a protocol power it will be necessary to find some modus vivendi with Karakhan when the group is handling protocol business. Those who know Karakhan best think this will not be difficult once he is given recognition as dean, the lack of which he has hitherto keenly felt. 5. Recently Karakhan sent the protocol powers a note protesting against the erection of hurdles by the American guard on what he called the “Russian glacis.” He was informed by powers in reply with appropriate references to the protocols of 190160 and 190461 that there was no “Russian glacis”; that the entire glacis was international property; that the American guard in holding the portion of it adjoining the Russian Legation property were merely discharging the duties which had been assigned them by the senior commandant and that equitation was a part of their military training.
