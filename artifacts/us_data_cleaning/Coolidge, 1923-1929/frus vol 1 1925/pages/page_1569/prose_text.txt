While I hope soon to be in a position to give more definite information as to the plans of the Chinese Government in regard to the conference it may be useful to the Department to know what I gather to be the tentative program of the administration. There has been worked out (apparently under the inspiration of W. W. Yen and Liang) a fairly coherent project involving the following points: A. Renunciation in principle by the treaty powers of all right of restriction upon the customs tariffs leviable by China. B. Undertaking by the Chinese Government to levy, at any rate for a specific period of years and after a transitional period of perhaps two or three years, graduated “scientific” tariff involving a free list and some four or five classifications up to rate of 30-odd percent on luxuries which my Chinese informants state would be in all tantamount to an average of between 8 and 9 percent on imports. C. Likin and other internal taxes on trade to be abolished forthwith.
