800.51 W 89Czechoslovakia/99: Telegram
[Document 73]
The Secretary of State to the Minister in Czechoslovakia (Einstein)
WASHINGTON, July 16, 1925—5 p.m.
28. Your 37, July 11, 1 P.M. On July 10 the following memorandum was handed to
Czechoslovak Chargé:
“The World War Foreign Debt Commission would be glad to negotiate upon
the settlement of the entire Czechoslovak debt to the United States with a
commission from Czechoslovakia having due power to negotiate and
conclude a settlement. Arrangements could be made in the debt settlement for
an appropriate adjustment of any part of the total indebtedness not now
admitted by Czechoslovakia to be due. There is no reason, however, why a
debt settlement need await a verification of the total since this is simply a
mechanical matter of auditing the accounts, and any debt settlement should
cover the entire debt whatever it is ultimately determined to be.”
Please keep Department informed by telegraph of further developments.
KELLOGG
