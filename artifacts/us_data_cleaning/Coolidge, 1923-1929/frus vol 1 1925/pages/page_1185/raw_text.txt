The Secretary of State to the Minister in China (Schurman)
WASHINGTON, February 28, 1925—4 p.m.
30. Your despatch No. 2590, November 8.57 The Department considers that the status
of the Soviet representative as Dean of the Diplomatic Body cannot be ignored and that
he is therefore entitled to participate in meetings of the Diplomatic Body, the scope of
whose deliberations, however, it may be anticipated will henceforth be limited to
matters of a purely formal or ceremonial character.
You are authorized whenever it may seem expedient to join your colleagues in
conveying to the Soviet representative an intimation that you are prepared to cooperate
with him in such matters as remain of general concern to the entire Diplomatic Body.
In view of the provisions of Articles III and XII of the Sino- Russian agreement of May
31, 1924,58 it would not appear necessary or appropriate that he should be admitted to
participation in meetings of the foreign ministers dealing with questions of treaty rights
such as those established by the Boxer protocol59 or with respect to extraterritoriality.
It is considered that the custody of the archives of the Diplomatic Body, with the
exception of those which are concerned with the deliberations of the whole Diplomatic
Body, as above referred to, should remain with the Senior Minister.
HUGHES
