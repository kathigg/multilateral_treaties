The Ambassador in Great Britain (Houghton) to the Secretary of State
LONDON, September 1, 1925—4 p.m.
[Received September 1—12:34 p.m.]
278. Department’s 282, August 31, 8 p.m. was read to Foreign Office whereupon
Embassy was informally advised that British Chargé d’Affaires at Peking would be
instructed to cooperate with his American colleague: First, in arranging McEuen’s
immediate suspension; second, in endeavoring to obtain as satisfactory assurances as
possible that McEuen will be retired in the event of his vindication; and third, in
expediting the holding of the judicial inquiry.
HOUGHTON
