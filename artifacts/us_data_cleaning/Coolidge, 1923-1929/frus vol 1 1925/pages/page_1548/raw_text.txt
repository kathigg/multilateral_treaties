The Chargé in China (Mayer) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, April 23, 1925—noon.
[Received April 23—7:26 a.m.]
167. Department’s 79, April 20, 3 p.m.38
1. Minister for Foreign Affairs states that according to present calculations,
and of course dependent upon French ratification of customs treaty39 which
French Minister forecasted to him should take place in about two weeks,40 he
desires to call Special Conference for September 1st at Peking. Minister for
Foreign Affairs seemed in some doubt whether it was for Chinese
Government to call the conference.
2. From second paragraph Department’s 69, April 6, 11 a.m.41 and from
Department’s 79, April 20, 3 p.m., I assume that the Department does not
find an obstacle to the convening of the Special Conference or cause for
embarrassment in that regard in the fact that the participating powers have
not recognized the Chinese Government de jure.
3. Special Conference not discussed at meeting of Heads of Legation of April
21st. See my 158, April 17, 6 p.m.38
MAYER
