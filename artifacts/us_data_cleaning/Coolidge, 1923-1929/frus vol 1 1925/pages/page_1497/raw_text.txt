793.00/105: Telegram
[Document 663]
The Minister in China (MacMurray) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, July 29, 1925—8 p.m.
[Received July 29—3:35 p.m.]
300. Your telegram number 170 July 28, 2 p.m. 1. I feel very strongly that phrase “tariff
autonomy” ought not to be used [garbled group]. As the sentence is phrased the
omission suggested by you would seem to imply opening Special Conference for the
reconsideration of all treaty matters including such questions as missionary rights and
the surrender of concessions and leased territories which the interested powers would
not of course consent to discuss. To make your meaning entirely definite might I not
substitute the words “treaty provisions dealing with customs duties” for “looking
toward ultimate tariff autonomy.”
MACMURRAY
