The Minister in Panama (South) to the Secretary of State
[Extract]
PANAMA, February 2, 1925.
[Received February 13.]
No. 628
SIR: Referring to the Department’s telegram No. 10 of January 23, 4 p.m., and
confirming my telegram No. 13 of January 30, 11 a.m.,24 I have the honor to transmit
herewith a copy of a note dated January 29, 1925 from the Minister for Foreign Affairs,
in which is set forth the attitude of the Panaman Government with regard to the
settlement of the Costa Rica boundary question. A translation of the above note is also
inclosed.
. . . . . . . . . . . . . .
This morning I received a note dated January 30, 1925, a copy and translation of which
are enclosed, informing me that the Government of Panama considers it essential that
some agreement along the lines specified in the communication of January 29th be
reached, before Panama can name its engineer to proceed with the demarcation of the
boundary line.
I have [etc.]
J. G. SOUTH
