463.00 R 29/163: Telegram [Document 132] The Ambassador in France (Herrick) to the Secretary of State PARIS, January 21, 1925—3 p.m. [Received 3:49 p.m.95 ] 75. Sargent96 has just sent me memorandum which British Embassy has been instructed to send in to the Ambassadors Conference: “The Reparation Commission recently fixed the contributions towards the cost of liberation referred to in the St. Germain Agreement of the 10th September 1919, modified by the statements of December 18 [8], 1919,97 as follows: Poland 225,495,000, gold; Roumania 235,140,000 gold francs; Serb-Croat-Slovene State 178,035,000 gold francs; Czechoslovakia 750,000,000 gold francs; total 1,388,670,- 000 gold francs. Italy 59,252,000 gold francs.98 This re-partition does not take into account any questions which may subsequently arise in regard to the territory of Fiume. In deciding to communicate the result of this re-partition and the above observation regarding the Fiume territory to the British, French and Italian Governments the Commission also decided to request these Governments to make known their opinion on the following points: (a) As to whether the United States of America should be consulted; (b) whether a formal request should be made for the issue of the bonds referred to in the agreements of 1919 in respect of the liberation debt; (c) if so to whom these bonds should be delivered. In the opinion of His Majesty’s Government the most convenient procedure would be for a decision on all these points to be reached by the Ambassadors Conference on behalf of the Governments concerned.
