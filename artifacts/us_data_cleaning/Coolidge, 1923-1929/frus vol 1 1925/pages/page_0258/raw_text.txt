500.A14/330: Telegram
[Document 46]
The Secretary of State to the Chairman of the American Delegation
(Burton)
WASHINGTON, June 15, 1925—11 a.m.
64. The Department has received your telegrams through No. 87, June 14, 9 p.m., and,
if in your judgment no new or objectionable features are introduced into the treaty prior
to the time of signature, and provided that a reasonable number of powers including
several of the principal arms producing nations are also prepared to sign, you are
authorized to sign the convention. Report at earliest possible moment for press release
both the hour of signature and who will sign for this Government, and send flash after
signature.
KELLOGG
