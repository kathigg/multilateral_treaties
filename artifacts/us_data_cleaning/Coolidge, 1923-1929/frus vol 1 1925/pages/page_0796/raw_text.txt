723.2515/1700: Telegram
[Document 257]
The Secretary of State to the Ambassador in Chile (Collier)
[Paraphrase]
WASHINGTON, December 1, 1925—noon.
77. Embassy’s 123, November 28, 8 p.m. I am greatly pleased to have your
information. In order that you may have background in any future conversations, I wish
to restate my position and explain attitude I took in my No. 69, October 31, 2 p.m.76
Nothing would be more gratifying to me than to have a diplomatic settlement of Tacna-
Arica question, and there has never been time when I would have hesitated to advise
both parties to make settlement of that sort. How to approach them is the difficult
question. I am not aware how I could do it without being asked by both parties, as I
indicated in my telegram No. 69, and without running great risk of criticism. As soon
as both parties ask my good offices, I am willing to advise settlement and to lend my
aid in every way.
KELLOGG
76 See footnote 73, p. 409 [Pg. 409 includes portions of Doc. 242 and Doc. 243].
