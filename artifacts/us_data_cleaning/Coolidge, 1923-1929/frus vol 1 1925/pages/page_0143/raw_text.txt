July 23
To the British Chargé
Assertion that the only point of difference among the powers
appears to be in regard to the precise phraseology of the reply to
the Chinese representations. Opinion that, as a matter of practical
policy, it would be preferable to convey fair warning to the
Chinese Government of its responsibility for the preservation of
order and other matters rather than to make complete restoration
of order a condition precedent to a discussion of reforms and
treaty relations. U. S. draft reply (text printed).
