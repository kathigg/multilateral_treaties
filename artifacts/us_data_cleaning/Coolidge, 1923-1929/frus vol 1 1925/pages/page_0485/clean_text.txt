463.00 R 29/181 [Document 148] The Unofficial Representative on the Reparation Commission (Hill) to the Secretary of State PARIS, 7 December, 1925. [Received December 16.] SIR: Supplementing my letter to the Department of October 30th last concerning the question of Liberation Bonds, (share of the cessionary States of the former Austro- Hungarian Monarchy), I have the honor to enclose for the Department’s information, two copies of a communication! dated December 5, 1925,17 transmitting a letter from the British Delegation to the Reparation Commission, dated December 3, 1925,17 in which it is stated that the British Government takes the view that it would be preferable not to call for the issue of the bonds in the present circumstances (F. S. Document 60– a). A copy of these documents has been transmitted to the Embassy here for its information. I am [etc.] RALPH W. S. HILL 17 Not printed. 17 Not printed.
