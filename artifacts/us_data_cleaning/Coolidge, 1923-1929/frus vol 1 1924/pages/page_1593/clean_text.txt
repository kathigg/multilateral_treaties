The President, as the Department has been informed, I understand, by the American Minister, is most favorably disposed to the most speedy settlement possible of this controversy but he, of course, cannot, in view of the financial situation of the Government, accept a settlement on a basis never foreseen by either party and a settlement to which his Government is in no wise obligated. If the company desires the matter adjusted, it should send Hunt to Santa Domingo immediately to negotiate directly with the Government. In addition please refer to my telegram of January 17, 11 a.m.41 The desired authorization should be granted immediately and should not be made in any sense contingent on a settlement of the controversy with the Santo Domingo Water, Light and Power Company. Unless the authorization is granted at once, all public works will cease next April with disastrous results not only with regard to the economic condition of the Republic, but to the whole question of our policy here.
