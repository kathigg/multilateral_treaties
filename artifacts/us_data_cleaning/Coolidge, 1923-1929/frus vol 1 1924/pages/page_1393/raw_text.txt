893.74/425: Telegram
[Document 550]
The Acting Secretary of State to the Minister in China
(Schurman)
[Paraphrase]
WASHINGTON, January 24, 1924—8 p.m.
25. With reference to your telegram 5 of January 4, Schwerin6 informs
Department that he would be very glad if a solution could be found which
would end the continued agitation to which the Chinese are subjected
because of the Federal contract. He does not believe, however, that the
Federal Telegraph Company can take over the Mitsui station at Peking to
relieve the Japanese of a bad bargain. He says that should his company try
to unite with the Japanese, the latter would insist upon sharing in the
activities of all the company’s stations. He also says that this would nullify
all the efforts of his company to give the American and Chinese people the
right of communication with each other without interference from any other
country.
Schwerin says he cannot go back to China before March.
PHILLIPS
6 R. P. Schwerin, president of the Federal Telegraph Co. of Delaware.
