893.00/5786: Telegram
[Document 365]
The Consul General at Shanghai (Cunningham) to the Secretary
of State
SHANGHAI, November 22, 1924.
[Received November 22—1:45 p.m.]
Following from Nanking:
“November 22, 11 a.m. Please send following telegram to the
Department: ‘Marshal Chi embarrassed by Wu’s unauthorized use
of his name in manifesto proclaiming Yangtze military
government, and has repeated circular telegram endorsing Tuan.
He will assist Wu defensively but considers aggression
impracticable.
Situation delicate and uncertain. Davis.’ ”
CUNNINGHAM
