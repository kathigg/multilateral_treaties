The Secretary of State to the Consul at Geneva (Tuck)
WASHINGTON, December 5, 1924—4 p.m.
Your December 4, 11 A.M. For Porter.
The Department has received an Aide Mémoire from the British Embassy at
Washington dated December 3, reading as follows:
“The American Delegation to the Second Opium Conference
which is now proceeding at Geneva have submitted a number of
proposals including two which the British Delegate considers to
be outside the scope of the Conference.
The object of the Conference is to consider measures to limit—
(1) the manufacture of morphine, heroin, cocaine, etc., (2) the
amounts of raw opium and coca leaf to be imported for
manufacture and for other medicinal and scientific purposes and
(3) the production of raw opium and coca leaf for export to the
amount required for medicinal and scientific purposes.
The American proposals in question are—(1) that the contracting
parties shall control the production and distribution of raw opium
so that there will be no surplus available for purposes not strictly
medical or scientific; and (2) that the contracting parties, in whose
territories the use of prepared opium is temporarily permitted,
shall agree to reduce the importation by 10 per cent each year so
as to bring the use of opium to an end within 10 years.
