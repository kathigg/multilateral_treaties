I suggested to the Minister that Peru had profited a great deal in prestige, as
well as in economic stability, by the concessions which it had made in the
settlement of its boundary disputes with Bolivia and Brazil, and in the
prospective adjustment by arbitration, if necessary, of a similar dispute with
Ecuador; and the immense benefit that would accrue to the country by the
settlement of its dispute with Colombia,—a dispute which would
undoubtedly be reopened in an intensified form if the treaty should be
rejected.
From all I can learn, especially through conversations with the Minister of
Colombia in Peru, the rejection of the treaty by the Peruvian Congress, or
the failure of that body to ratify it, would cause very intense, hostile feeling
in the former country.
