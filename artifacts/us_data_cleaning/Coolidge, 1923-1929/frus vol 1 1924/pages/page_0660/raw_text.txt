811.2300/38: Telegram
[Document 172]
The Minister in China (Schurman) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, January 26, 1924—noon.
[Received January 26—6:37 a.m.]
46. Your 19, January 18, 7 p.m.99 Aviation Department object to granting
permission on the ground that, whereas, when in other instances permission
given for experimental flights across Chinese territory, governments
concerned had stated machines to be of nonmilitary character and flights
made for scientific purposes, in present instance no such information given.
Foreign Office states Chinese regulations prohibit military aeroplanes of
foreign nations flying over Chinese territory. Aviation Department holding
conference with representatives of general staff and Ministry of War on
January 30th to decide question.
I suggest you telegraph that aeroplanes to be used are of nonmilitary
character and flight for scientific purposes if such is the case.
SCHURMAN
99 Not printed.
