The Minister in Switzerland (Grew) to the Secretary of State
BERNE, February 8, 1924—8 p.m.
[Received 11:10 p.m.]
11. I regret that the Temporary Mixed Commission had adjourned before
your number 1, February 7, 7 p.m., was received.
With regard to the question of the private manufacture of arms, my
statement to the Commission on February 6th was as follows:
“The second point that I have in mind was a reference in this
Commission to the possible desirability of combining in one draft
convention the two questions of the control of the traffic in arms
and the control of the private manufacture of arms and of
munitions of war. So far as my own position on this subject is
concerned, I believe I should remind the members of this
Commission that my instructions do not authorize me to entertain
any subject other than the traffic in arms. It will be recalled that
the invitation addressed to the Government of the United States
on December 14th last28 mentioned this subject only and that it
was on this understanding that the Government accepted the
invitation in question.”
[Paraphrase.] I can inform the Commission that in view of the discussions
on February 6 I asked for further instructions from my Government
concerning its position with respect to the question of private production
and as far as seems desirable I can set forth the views of the Department.
There are two ways by which I suggest that this might be done: (1) I can
write to the Secretary General of the League of Nations that my
Government declines to participate in the meeting of the subcommittee on
March 24 in Paris and in the same letter I can ask him to convey these
further views of my Government; (2) we can be represented in the meeting
