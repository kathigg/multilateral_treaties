893.51/4704: Telegram
[Document 383]
The Chargé in China (Bell) to the Secretary of State
[Extracts]
PEKING, October 22, 1924—4 p.m.
[Received October 22—9:55 a.m.]
398. My 397, October 20, 5 p.m. 1. Following from American consul
Canton:
“October 21, 3 p.m. Your October 20, 5 p.m. Three British and
two French river gunboats now in port, also United States ship
Helena and Pampanga. United States ship Sacramento at
Hongkong. Understand that Russian cruiser Vorowsky still at
Whampoa. Japanese consul tells me he will not ask for naval
vessel until necessity evident.
. . . . . . . . . . . . . .
Understand that everything essential in customhouse has already
been transferred to Shameen and that commissioner could carry
on from concession even if Sun seized customs building. Such
course might be preferable to forcible measures by the powers.”
. . . . . . . . . . . . . .
BELL
