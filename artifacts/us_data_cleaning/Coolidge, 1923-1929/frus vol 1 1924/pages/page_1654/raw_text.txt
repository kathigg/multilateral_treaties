The Government of the United States furthermore confidently
expects that the export tax on cacao will be continued after its
expiration December 31, 1925, until the debt is fully satisfied as
was promised by the President of Ecuador in the note above
referred to.
The question of the debt of the Association of Agriculturists to the
Mercantile Bank of the Americas has been pending for a number
of years without any efficacious action on the part of the
Ecuadorean Government and the Government of the United
States feels that it must now ask for a prompt settlement and
desires to be informed without delay of the steps contemplated by
the Ecuadorean Government to bring the matter to a satisfactory
conclusion.”
A copy of the above note has been handed to the Ecuadorean Minister in
Washington and he has been informed that this Government is wholly
dissatisfied with the attitude and action of the Ecuadorean authorities in the
premises and that this Government feels that a settlement of the matter
cannot well be prolonged and it expects to be advised without delay of the
intentions of the Ecuadorean Government in the premises.
