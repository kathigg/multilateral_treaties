From a survey of the actual working of the Agreement of May, 1919, it is
the view of this Government that the precise formula in use has been of less
importance than the intent and spirit of the various governments concerned
in carrying out the Agreement entered into at that time. In spite of the
infractions of the embargo which have occurred from time to time, this
Government is of the opinion that the embargo has attained a measurable
success, and it desires in every way to strengthen its effectiveness. In view,
however, of the difficulties apparently to be met at the present time in
substituting the revised Washington formula (with the interpretative note)
for the Agreement of May, 1919, this Government questions whether any
practical benefits would be derived from an attempt to do so until a more
complete unanimity can be had among the interested Powers. I need hardly
say that I regret that no greater success has thus far attended the efforts
which have been made to procure substantial unanimity in this matter.
Accept [etc.]
CHARLES E. HUGHES
