Memorandum by the Acting Chief of the Division of Far Eastern
Affairs, Department of State (Lockhart)
[WASHINGTON,] July 31, 1924.
Mr. Butler Wright telephoned me today stating that Signor Rosso of the
Italian Embassy had today informed him that the Embassy had received
information from Rome to the effect that the Italian Government had
decided not to raise its Legation at Peking to the rank of an Embassy, and
that the decision had been arrived at for reasons of economy. Mr. Wright
suggested that this information be made of record in order that it might be
considered in connection with the general question of raising the American
Legation at Peking to an Embassy.
F. P. L[OCKHART]
