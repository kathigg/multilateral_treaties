The Minister in China (Schurman) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, July 30, 1924—7 p.m.
[Received July 30—10:53 a.m.]
268. Your 156, July 15, 7 a.m. [6 p.m.] Following telegram was sent today
to consul Harbin:
“July 30, 6 p.m. My July 22, 1 p.m. Four Ministers today agreed
on following telegram to Harbin consuls.
‘While the Ministers accept the conclusion of the
consuls that the present time is not opportune for the
removal of the seals, they desire to impress the consuls
with their conviction that the seals should be removed
at the earliest practicable date. As to this date they
would desire to be advised by the consuls’.
For your knowledge and guidance I would say that I am very
desirous of the early removal of the seals which is the instruction
of the Department.”
SCHURMAN
