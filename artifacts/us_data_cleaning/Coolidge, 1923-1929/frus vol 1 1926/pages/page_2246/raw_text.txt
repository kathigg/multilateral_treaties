[2.] Telegram to Shanghai refers to the fact that all present at the meeting
except Italian Minister severally declared themselves willing to take such
action. For my part, while regretting such a breach of solidarity among the
powers, I shall have no hesitation in taking such action rather than let local
Italian interests at Shanghai hold us to ransom. Italian Minister shows some
indications of weakening in the face of united determination of all his
colleagues but may not receive in time for tomorrow’s meeting instructions
he insists will be necessary to authorize change in his stand.
3. Consul general at Shanghai has now reported that a meeting of the
consular body December 26th rejected the proposal outlined in the second
paragraph of my telegram to him but considered and is referring to the
Ministers’ two other possible compromises, both of which however failed to
receive support of the Italian consul general.
MACMURRAY
