The Minister in China (MacMurray) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, December 15, 1926—3 p.m.
[Received December 15—7:20 a.m.]
615. Following from American consul general at Shanghai:
“December 14, 4 p.m. Fifteen hundred men being the vanguard
Southern forces have invaded Chekiang from Kiangsi and are
advancing unopposed on Hangchow. Declaration of independence
of Chekiang momentarily expected. If it is accompanied by
expulsion of Southern[ers], it is believed Sun Ch’uan-fang will
acquiesce; otherwise, Sun expected to move loyal forces into
Chekiang to meet Southern [forces]. His forces on Chekiang
border rapidly being strengthened.”
It is understood that Southern Nationalists will not assent to making
Chekiang a buffer region but are determined to occupy it.
MACMURRAY
