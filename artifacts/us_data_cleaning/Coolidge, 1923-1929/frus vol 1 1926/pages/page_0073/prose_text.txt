For Lassiter: Instructions not to press for resolution charging Chile with frustration, but to continue in attitude of withholding judgment and of proceeding with plebiscite, and to make no intimation of what may be ultimate action. [Pg. includes portions of Doc. and Doc. 161] Feb. 18 (20) From the Ambassador in Chile (tel.) Belief that diplomatic settlement can be brought about, despite recent change in sentiment which now favors plebiscite. Peruvian President’s response, to inquiry made by head of New York house of Wessel, Duval & Co. as to a peaceable compromise, that bitterness could be ended only by restoration of the provinces to Peru.
