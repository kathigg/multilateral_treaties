From the Minister in China (tel.)
Report of a conversation with Acting Minister for
Foreign Affairs, August 18, which seemed to give some
hope of an understanding; assertion that, in view of the
indeterminate status of the Cabinet, no more categorical
statement was made than to intimate that the moment is
nearing when the Radio Corporation will have to make
arrangements elsewhere if the Chinese fail to make use
of the opportunity open to them.

Aug. 27 From the British Ambassador
Memorandum (text printed) giving reasons why the
British Government considers that the Federal contract
involves a virtual monopoly; statement that, if the
Federal contract is carried out, the British Government
may be obliged to take concerted action with the other
powers concerned for the protection of British interests.
