893.5045/385: Telegram
[Document 601]
The Chargé in China (Mayer) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, September 24, 1926—3 p.m.
[Received September 24—10:05 a.m.]
429. My 419, September 20, 8 [5] p.m. Following from American consul
general, Canton:
“September 23, 4 p.m. Referring to my telegram of September 19,
4 p.m. There were no pickets at Shameen gates today and
indications are that boycott as constituted during the last year is
coming to end. It seems certain however that British
manufactured goods will still be under the ban. Antiforeign
feeling continues very widespread.”
MAYER
