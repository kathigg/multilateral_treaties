The Minister in China (MacMurray) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, September 1, 1926—7 p.m.
[Received September 1—1:45 p.m.]
366. My 360, August 30, 5 p.m. Following from American consul general at
Hankow:
“Concessions here overcrowded with refugees. Frightened
Chinese from the native city still coming in, creating great
concern and resulting in much confusion. They are much alarmed,
not at the prospect apparently of the Southern troops arriving but
of looting by Northern troops. In case of trouble in the
concession, foreign volunteer forces and naval units will be
utilized, in cooperation with the Chinese authorities, in protecting
life and property. Chinese authorities have made such a request of
the foreign consuls. Wu is known to be in desperate straits across
the river and this is having an unfavorable reaction here.”
MACMURRAY
