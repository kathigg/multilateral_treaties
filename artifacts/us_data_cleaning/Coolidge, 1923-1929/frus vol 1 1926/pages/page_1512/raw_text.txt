893.00/7748: Telegram
[Document 509]
The Chargé in China (Mayer) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, October 15, 1926—11 a.m.
[Received October 15—3: 50 a.m.]
481. My 472, October 12, 5 p.m. Following from American consul general,
Hankow:
“October 14, 3 p.m. French gunboat Alerte fired upon yesterday
near Wusüeh, killing one sailor, seriously injuring another. Alerte
fired upon again below Hankow this morning but no casualties.
Fire returned both times.
Conditions at Wuchang continue to improve. Shops are opening
and food supplies ample. About 400 bodies inside and outside
walls in crude coffins now awaiting burial. All Northern troops
evacuated.
No change in the situation at Kiukiang.”
MAYER
