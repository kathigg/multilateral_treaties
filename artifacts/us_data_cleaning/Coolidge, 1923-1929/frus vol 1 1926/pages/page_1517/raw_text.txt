The Minister in China (MacMurray) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, November 8, 1926—noon.
[Received November 8—2:48 a.m.]
534. My 553 [533], November 6, 11 a.m. Following from American consul
general, Hankow:
“November 6, 1 p.m. American naval radio from Kiukiang this
morning states city quiet and completely occupied by Southern
troops, practically all Northerners having been evacuated down
river. Japanese and American landing forces withdrawn. Fighting
reported in vicinity of Wusüeh.”
MACMURRAY
