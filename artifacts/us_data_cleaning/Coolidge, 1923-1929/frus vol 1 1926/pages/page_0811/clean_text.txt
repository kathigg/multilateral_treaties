which were cabled to you, that the Government of the United States not only has no authority to bring pressure by force directly or indirectly upon either Chile or Peru but also does not entertain the slightest intention of doing so. The Plebiscitary Commission can not be supported and will not be supported by any form of forcible intervention from the United States. Under no circumstances would the leverage of a naval visit or other manifestation of force be contemplated. In the third place, in the event of a settlement this Government will not join in guarantees of any kind, political or economic. I had hoped to have Peru’s reply before now. If you have not already presented memorandum, please do so at once, and cable reply at earliest possible moment. KELLOGG
