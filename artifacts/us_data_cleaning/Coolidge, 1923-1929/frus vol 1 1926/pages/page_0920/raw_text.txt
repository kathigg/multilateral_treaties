723.2515/2084a: Telegram
[Document 222]
The Secretary of State to the Consul at Arica (Von Tresckow)
[Paraphrase]
WASHINGTON, April 1, 1926—10 a.m.
For Lassiter.
(1) Current press reports state that Peruvian voters who were sent
into the plebiscitary territory a few weeks ago to register are now
being sent out without their having been registered. I have
understood that five or six hundred Peruvian voters have for
several weeks been encamped near Arica. I should like to be
advised of reasons given by Peruvian delegate for removal of
these candidates for registration if they are now being removed. It
is also our understanding that there are hundreds of other voters in
the territory who have not yet tried to register. Inquiry made to
Ambassador Velarde and his counsel for reason for this
nonregistration has failed to elicit any satisfactory answer. Please
report fully.
(2) I have also been informed that President Leguía is thinking of
sending Freyre here as Peruvian Plenipotentiary; if this were
realized, negotiations would be delayed for at least four weeks
more.
KELLOGG
