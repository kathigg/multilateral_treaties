It should be added that the attitude taken by the United States Government in connection with the claims of the Mitsui Company for the exclusive right to establish radio stations in China is similar to the attitude assumed by it in connection with the contract entered into by the Great Northern Telegraph Company and the Chinese Government, whereby the former claimed a monopoly of cable communications in China.82 The Japanese Government stated in its memorandum of June 1, 1925, that it appeared that the American counter-project was intended to secure for the Federal Company the exclusive control of all radio stations to be constructed by the Company.
