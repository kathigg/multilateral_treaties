The Ambassador in Chile (Collier) to the Secretary of State
SANTIAGO, June 8, 1926—1 p.m.
[Received 3:10 p.m.]
201. After a talk with the Under Secretary and consideration of the action of
last night’s meeting of notables, I consider a diplomatic solution hopeless
and feel that Lassiter ought not to delay a moment longer.
The element here which fails to appreciate the seriousness of a solution
condemning Chile for electoral frauds rallied all the jingoists and succeeded
in overcoming the great number of influential persons who wanted a
diplomatic settlement. For instance, the Mercurio has a headline today
running across the page “Only the sons of the heroes are worthy of guarding
the sepulchre of the martyrs,” and subheading “This is the voice of the
Chileans who with their blood bought Tacna and Arica.”
There was unquestionably a set-back yesterday to those who were working
for a diplomatic settlement, a sort of mysterious slump. …
I keenly regret that we are not to have the satisfaction of bringing Chile and
Peru into better relations and personally am confident that Chile has yielded
to influence which has brought about results that I think will be disastrous
for her; but you and I certainly have both worked and endured in order to
aid Chile, and I think our Government at least will have the satisfaction of
having kept faith and that Lassiter’s resolution will be a new declaration of
our high standing of honor which must be kept unimpaired if we are to have
any influence in Latin America or the rest of the world or if we are to
maintain our own self-respect.
COLLIER
