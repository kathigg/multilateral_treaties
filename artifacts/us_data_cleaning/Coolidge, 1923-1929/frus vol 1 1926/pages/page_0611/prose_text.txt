However, a pilgrim ship, if it has had no plague or cholera patients during the course of the voyage from Djeddah to Yambo or Tor and if the individual medical examination made at Tor after debarkation establishes the fact that it contains no such patients, may be authorized by the Sanitary Maritime and Quarantine Board of Egypt to pass through the Suez Canal in quarantine even at night when the following four conditions are fulfilled: 1. Medical attendance shall be given on board by one or several physicians graduated and duly accepted; 2. The vessel shall be provided with disinfecting chambers in good working order; 3. It shall be shown that the number of pilgrims does not exceed that authorized by the pilgrimage regulations; 4. The captain shall bind himself to repair directly to the port which he names as his next call port. The sanitary tax to be paid to the Quarantine administration shall be the same as the pilgrims would have paid had they remained in quarantine three days. ARTICLE 143 A vessel which has had a suspicious case on board during the voyage from Tor to Suez may be sent back to Tor.

ARTICLE 144 The transshipment of pilgrims is strictly forbidden in Egyptian ports except by special permit and on the conditions laid down by the Egyptian Sanitary authority in accord with the Maritime Sanitary and Quarantine Board of Egypt.
