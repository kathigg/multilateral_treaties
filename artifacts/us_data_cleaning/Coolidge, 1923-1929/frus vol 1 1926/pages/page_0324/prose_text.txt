(4) It is true that in the case of this Government the time was brief, as it has not participated in the meetings which preceded the invitation and as it received the documentation necessary for the preparation of instructions only a short time before the meeting was called. Work on preparation of instructions is now proceeding, however, in consultation with War and Navy Departments and will be completed in ample time to send the instructions to you before February 15. If other powers should now desire postponement, we do not object; but we do not propose a postponement and the onus for one should not be placed on us. (5) As you are aware, the Department does not intend to send large delegation to Geneva and probably only technical military and naval assistants will be sent from here, consisting probably of an admiral and a general with possibly technical aides. (6) At your discretion you may indicate orally to Drummond49 substance of paragraphs (2) and (3); you may add that it is probable that any personnel which may be sent from here will be of technical character.
