The American Delegation to the Secretary of State
PEKING, March 3, 1926—3 p.m.
[Received March 3—9:43 a.m.]
Conference 26. British delegates inactive, say they are waiting instructions
from their Government respecting the attitude of Chinese provinces toward
Conference and proposed treaty. We told them we proposed to proceed with
negotiations irrespective disturbed conditions; that we were bound to
effectuate Washington Treaty and thought it advantageous that powers
should endeavor to get in accord on general tariff policy toward China, then
if China went to pieces before treaty ratified, responsibility of disintegration
would not be on powers. Under all the circumstances we believe we should
diligently proceed with negotiations and encourage others to do so.
AM[ERICAN] TAR [IFF] DEL[EGATION]
