In declaring the plebiscite impracticable, a finding being based on
antecedents which were not communicated to the accused Party, the
aforesaid parallelism disappears and, therefore, I have been instructed by
my Government to bring to an end the negotiations under the good
office[s].
 
I am especially pleased to convey the sincere thanks of the Chilean
Government for the constant and disinterested services which have been
lent, to the cause of harmony and concord among American countries, by
the Honorable The Secretary of State, whose intelligent efforts were set into
action without attaining the desired results due to the fact that the
Resolution of the majority of the Plebiscitary Commission brought about
the premature end of the negotiations in which he was so nobly engaged.
WASHINGTON , June 18, 1926.
32a Made in the Chilean Embassy.
