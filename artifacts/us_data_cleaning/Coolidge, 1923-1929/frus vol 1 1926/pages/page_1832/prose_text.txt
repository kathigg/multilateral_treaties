Practically all of the work of fixing the rates of surtaxes may be said to have been done in informal conferences among the Delegates and Technical Advisers. To have attempted to thresh out these questions in formal sittings of the Delegates or Advisers would have been a prolonged, and perhaps futile, task and the expeditious and satisfactory manner in which it was handled does great credit to the Technical Advisers who bore the brunt of this work. Much of it was of a technical character and involved a knowledge of values and commodities which some of the Delegates could not be expected to possess. It was for this reason that it was deemed advisable to leave this work largely to the Japanese, British and American Advisers.
