See in this connection telegram No. 273 from the Department, November 19, 4 p.m.,28 which communicated the telegram received from London28 in which is contained Wellesley’s opinion that the better course for the treaty powers would be to grant the additional surtax of 2½ percent at once and without reservations, the surtax to be collected if possible by the Customs Administration. You should consult your British and French colleagues for the purpose of getting, if possible, agreement upon joint action. It should be possible, it seems to the Department, for the Powers simply to give notification to the Chinese Government that an authorization is given for the surtaxes to be collected through the Chinese Maritime Customs.
