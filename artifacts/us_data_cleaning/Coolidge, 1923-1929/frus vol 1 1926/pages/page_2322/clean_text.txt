Pending the arrival of Yoshizawa, at which time presumably it will develop whether definite proposals are contemplated by the Japanese, I suggest that it is inadvisable to communicate to Radio Corporation this recent phase of the situation unless doing so is necessary to keep alive the interest of the corporation in the matter, since, until the matter is further in progress, for them to send their representatives here would hardly serve any useful purpose or be justified. However, should the Department give information of the matter to Radio Corporation, may I suggest that it be given somewhat in this sense? MAYER
