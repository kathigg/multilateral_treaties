In the present case it seems clear that Chu Shea-wai, although he is a citizen of the United States, under the law of this country, because of the fact that he was born in this country, is also a citizen of China, under the law of that country, because his father was of Chinese nationality, and it does not appear that the existence of extraterritorial jurisdiction in China interferes in any way with the right of China to claim this individual as a Chinese citizen.
