The Chargé in China (Mayer) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, October 12, 1926—4 p.m.
[Received October 12—9:35 a.m.]
471. My 466, October 9, 4 p.m.36 Following from American consul general
at Canton:
“October 11, 2 p.m. Kuomintang and strike committee issued
lengthy statements yesterday declaring boycott ended but
reaffirming intention to carry into effect ‘economic struggle’ with
renewed vigor against the British and imperialists generally
throughout China. A few Chinese came on the Island37 today but
the general public seems very slow to resume pre-strike relations,
and it is still too early to judge the situation. There were no
disorders.”
MAYER
36 Not printed.
37 Shameen.
