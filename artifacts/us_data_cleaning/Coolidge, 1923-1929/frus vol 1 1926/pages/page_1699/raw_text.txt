The Secretary of State to the Minister in China (MacMurray)
WASHINGTON, April 13, 1926—3 p.m.
80. Your 162, April 3, 2 p.m. While inclined to agree with you that present
arms embargo has failed materially to restrict the supply of arms to warring
factions in China, the Department does not believe that the United States
should take the lead in recommending its revision or discontinuance,
especially at the present time when to do so would offer seeming
justification for a charge that we desired to favor one faction against
another. The Department would therefor prefer that you refrain for the
present from initiating any discussion of question.
KELLOGG
