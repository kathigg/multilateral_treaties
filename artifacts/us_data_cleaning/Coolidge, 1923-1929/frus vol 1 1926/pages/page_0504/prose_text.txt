Lieutenant-Commander J. L. Callan, U. S. N. As a result of the deliberations of the Aeronautical Commission a Convention was prepared and was signed by certain countries on October 13, 1919. The Convention lays down certain rules for the regulation of aircraft engaged in international traffic. Annexes to the Convention prescribe a number of technical rules and regulations regarding the marking of planes, the granting of certificates of airworthiness, qualifications of pilots, signals and other requirements intended to promote safety in international traffic by aircraft.
