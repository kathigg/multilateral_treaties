Authority of the Commission and general arrangement for holding the plebiscite should be maintained unimpaired. There is no certainty that Chile would accept foregoing plan; but the possibility of her accepting it is such, provided Peru is likely to accept, that I think it would be a wise procedure for you to sound out Ambassador Cruchaga. If on Tuesday, April 27, or before then, he were to suggest or even to report this plan to Chilean Government for its consideration, such action might avert resolution by Chamber of Deputies, on April 28, to terminate good offices. . . . . . . . . . . . . . .
