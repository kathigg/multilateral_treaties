The Minister in China (MacMurray) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, September 1, 1926—noon.
[Received September 1—5:51 a.m.]
362. My 357, August 27, 3 p.m.
1. Following from American consul general, Hankoitem:
“August 31, 10 a.m. Wu Pei-fu has suffered serious setback at the
hands of the Southerners and has fallen back early this morning.
Southern troops now very near Wuchang. Admiral Hough20
returning today via commercial transport. Isabel in Shanghai for
overhauling; Polos and Portage [Pigeon?] now here. Suggest that
destroyers be sent here at once as a precautionary measure since
there is some danger of looting from the retreating forces. Local
American community requests this measure of security.
2. I concur in suggestion regarding despatch of destroyers to Hankow and
have so informed commander in chief, United States Asiatic Fleet,
repeating to him Lockhart’s telegram aforementioned.
MACMURRAY
20 Rear Admiral Henry H. Hough, U. S. N., commander of the Yangtze
Patrol.
