I am strongly in favor of informing the British Government that we have taken this position, which would involve action on our part, so far as protecting American citizens might require, similar to but not as drastic as that of the British, though it would not associate us with British instructions to British naval forces at Canton and would not be publicly cooperative. 6. I may add with reference to last sentence of second paragraph of my telegram 326 of August 14,32 that while I do believe that in many instances it is wise for us to act in accord with the British, and the Japanese as well, in China, this should in my opinion be mainly cooperation as to understanding and complete information with regard to the plans and policies of each other, with a view rather to simultaneous action than to our direct and public association with them in joint action.
