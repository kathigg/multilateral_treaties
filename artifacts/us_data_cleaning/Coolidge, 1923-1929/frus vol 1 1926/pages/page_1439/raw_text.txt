The Minister in China (MacMurray) to the Secretary of State
PEKING, July 26, 1926—noon.
[Received July 26—7:55 a.m.]
304. Following from American consul general at Canton:
“July 23, noon. I assume Changsha and Hankow consuls are able
to keep you advised respecting military situation. Canton army
appears to be advancing rapidly and local leaders are now
predicting early capture of Hankow and Wuchang. General
Chiang15 of Whampoa Cadets is expected to leave Canton in a
few days for Hunan.”
MACMURRAY
15 Chiang Kai-shek, commander in chief of the Cantonese Northern
Expedition; also known as Chiang Kai-shih and Chiang Chung-cheng.
