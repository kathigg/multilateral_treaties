b. It is a clean, simple solution free from obvious
complicating factors attendant upon other plans.
c. It is comprehensive and definitive, leaving no room
whatever for claims and disputes, and maneuvers for
revision of territorial dispositions.
d. It can injure no national susceptibilities, either
Chilean or Peruvian. Neither country makes any
concession to the other and the moral positions of both,
so far as the original controversy is concerned, are left
intact.
e. It takes into account the continental interest in the
controversy and embodies a settlement which South
America as a whole could welcome as one insuring
permanent peace and stability.
Frank B. Kellogg
Secretary of State.
Washington, November 30, 1926.”
You are also instructed to cable the Department immediately the hour of
memorandum’s delivery and a full account of your interview.
KELLOGG
