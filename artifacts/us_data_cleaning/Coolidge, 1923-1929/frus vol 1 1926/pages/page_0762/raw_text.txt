The Consul at Arica (Von Tresckow) to the Secretary of State
ARICA, January 18, 1926—6 p.m.
[Received January 19—12:35 a.m.]
From Pershing.
1. With reference to my contemplated absence from Arica and
from duty in connection with the Plebiscitary Commission,
Tacna-Arica Arbitration on account of illness and further
reference to Arbitrator’s ruling that no one can be designated as
commissioner ad interim, I hereby tender my resignation as
commissioner in order that an American member may be
appointed in my place.
2. I consider it important that the president of the Commission be
here in the territory or near at hand at all times and therefore
recommend that my resignation be accepted to take effect upon
my departure from Arica and that General Lassiter be appointed
to take effect at the same time.
3. General Lassiter is expected to arrive on the 21st and I shall
leave as soon thereafter as practicable and shall notify you the
exact date when determined.91
VON TRESCKOW
91 General Pershing left Arica Jan. 27, 1926.
