(3) I am pleased that your relations with both parties are cordial and I hope
they will remain so, for your personal relation at this juncture is supremely
important. I feel that your position will be almost impregnable through
maintaining scrupulously impartial and judicial attitude towards both
parties.
(4) It is probable that the investigations which have been pursued in the past
few months were necessary in order to permit intelligent formation of rules
and regulations. As you know, these investigations and manner of making
them have been subject of bitter criticism by one of parties. I am not
disposed to justify this criticism, but I suggest whether you will not be able
(in addition to keeping generally informed about what is going on) to place
upon the power charged with the administration of the territory the
responsibility of maintaining order and to impress upon it the consequences
of any failure.
KELLOGG
