The Secretary of State to the Chargé in China (Mayer)
WASHINGTON, October 9, 1926—2 p.m.
224. Press reports dreadful suffering civilian population in Wuchang. You
are instructed to correspond with Lockhart in the endeavor to ascertain
whether we can be of assistance.
Your 465, October 9, 3 p.m., just received. Should situation of civilian
population in Wuchang become such as to seem to justify it, it is suggested
that consular body in Hankow endeavor to arrange armistice, during which
some means of relief might be devised. Red Cross would be disposed to
assist in case of necessity. Wire your recommendation.
KELLOGG
