Bill Sebald is working with Judge Phleger on additional material on the renunciation of use of force. This material is aimed for your use in the course of detailed discussions on the item, assuming that a detailed discussion stage will be reached eventually. The foregoing thoughts on the missing personnel item and the trade embargo are of course subject to confirmation by official tele­ gram. We have not yet cleared with the Secretary. I presume O’Neill will put your three questions formally to the Chinese Communists today or tomorrow. The Foreign Office instructed him to do so (subject to his concurrence) on October 7. We have told the British we do not like O’Neill’s suggestion that he refer to the PRC promise to notify him of the result of trials of Amer­ icans, and inform the PRC authorities that he looks forward to early notification of reduction of sentence in all cases. This sounds too much like accepting the PRC thesis, and giving up on the implementation of the Agreed Announcement. We are looking forward to the arrival of Ralph Clough this after­ noon. No doubt we will get a better feel of the current atmosphere from him, although I must say that your telegrams have given us a very full and complete picture. We have felt that we have almost had a front center seat. Regards, Sincerely, Walter P. McConaughy 279. Telegram 865 to Geneva1 Washington, October 11, 1955, 6:49 p.m. 865. For Johnson.
