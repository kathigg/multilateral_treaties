The Secretary is still taking a firm position against travel of Ameri­ can correspondents to Communist China while the 19 are still held. He reaffirmed this position to a CBS Executive recently. The pressure for immediate relaxation is undiminished. The pouch is closing. We are meeting this afternoon on the guid­ ance for your Thursday meeting. We hope to get this out tomorrow so you will have time to assimilate the telegram and come back at us if necessary before your meeting. You did well to get the meetings established on a weekly schedule without any adverse reaction from Wang. Krishna Menon saw the Secretary alone on the 15th. I do not have any report on what transpired yet. I hope we can send a report on any­ thing of direct interest within the next few days. Good wishes, Sincerely, Walter P.
