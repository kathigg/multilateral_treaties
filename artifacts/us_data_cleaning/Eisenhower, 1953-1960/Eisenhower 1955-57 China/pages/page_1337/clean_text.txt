Letter from Osborn to Clough1 Geneva, December 26, 1956 Dear Ralph: I am returning the extra copies of the missing-­personnel dossiers you mentioned in your letter of December 19. As you say, we did get data on twelve persons. It should be possible to make a pretty good presentation on the basis of the material we now have, if we can have discretion as to the use of material from some of the classified dossiers. Although some of the new material is still utterly valueless in our context (e.g., Pvt Worley, Lt. Gleaves, Lt De Lacy, Lt Cochran, Lt Batson, Lt Nelson), I don’t think this would necessarily hurt our presentation. We now have a few cases we could cite in some detail to cast doubt on the so-­called “accounting” in both the “escaped” and the “no-­data” categories (e.g., Pvt Walker, Lt Biesterveld, Capt Moreland—­why have we no new data on him?—­and several of the Air Force cases).
