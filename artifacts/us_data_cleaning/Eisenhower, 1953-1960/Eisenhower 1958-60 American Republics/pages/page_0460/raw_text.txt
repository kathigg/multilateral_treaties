President Lleras said he had been trying to modernize the Colombian 
army to deal with the dangerous guerilla problem (see Memorandum, 
same conversation, “Role of the Military”),2 and that he had thought of 
creating two or three special battalions, but that his military officers were 
arch-­conservative in their outlook and methods.
He said the United States had sent a survey mission that had, in its 
preliminary report,3 “recommended some conventional methods that 
I don’t think will solve the problem”. He said he would be glad to see 
the final report.
President Lleras emphasized the Colombian military’s need for 
rapid transport. To be effective, anti-­guerilla forces must arrive at 
the scene of danger or violence rapidly, not “two days later to pick 
up the dead”. He had thought of helicopters. Mr. Rubottom said he 
understood that helio-­couriers, with short take-­off and heavy carrying 
capacity, were less vulnerable than helicopters and more suited to the 
work in Colombia, apart from being far cheaper. He said one had been 
offered to the Colombian Government on loan, and was waiting orders 
at Brownsville.
President Lleras agreed with the preliminary report’s emphasis 
on the need for improving the Colombian Government’s intelligence. 
He suggested that Colombia might send up some men for training, 
and he was assured this could be arranged. After another mention 
by Mr. Rubottom of the availability of the helio-­courier plane, the 
President indicated willingness to have his Government receive it for 
trial.
