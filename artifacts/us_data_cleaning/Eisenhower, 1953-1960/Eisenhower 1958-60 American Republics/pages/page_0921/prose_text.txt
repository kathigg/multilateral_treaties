Standard is obligated to prepay charter fees if the borrower is unable to make payments on the ship loans. If Standard were to halt operations, there would be no charters for the ships, thus no payment on the ship loans, and the sale of the ships would not realize the amount of the outstanding loan balances on them. He added that the bank was looking for a purchaser for Standard’s collateral, stock in two breweries and a soap factory, but that it might take some time to find a satisfactory purchaser. He said that no matter how Standard’s financial problems may be resolved, he felt that the company should rid itself of the breweries and soap factory. The two crucial questions regarding Standard’s future in the bank’s eyes are how to overcome the political risks in Honduras, and will it be possible for Standard to stop its current losses. As matters stand, the company will be unable to meet its payrolls by the middle of January.
