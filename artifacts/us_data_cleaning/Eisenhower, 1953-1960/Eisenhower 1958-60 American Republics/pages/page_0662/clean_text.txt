Editorial Note By an exchange of notes, dated April 23 and November 17, 1958, respectively, El Salvador and the United States agreed, effective November 17, to extend for four years the existing agreement providing for a United States Army Mission to El Salvador. Copies of the notes were enclosed in despatch 317 from El Salvador, December 10. (716.58/12–1058) For text of the agreement, see 9 UST 1484. ES–4. Editorial Note President Lemus of El Salvador made a state visit to the United States, March 9–21, 1959. On March 11, he had discussions with Acting Secretary of State Herter and President Eisenhower. Memoranda of these conversations are printed herein. On the same day, Salvadoran Foreign Minister Ortíz Mancía and Economics Minister Rochac met with Assistant Secretary of State Rubottom. A memorandum of this conversation is in decimal file 816.10/3–1159. Additional documentation on Lemus’ visit is in decimal file 716.11; and Conference Files, Lot 64 D 560, CF 1207 and 1207A. For further information, see the Department of State Bulletin, March 9, 1959, page 330; and Public Papers of the Presidents of the United States: Dwight D. Eisenhower, 1959, pages 239, 241, and 253.
