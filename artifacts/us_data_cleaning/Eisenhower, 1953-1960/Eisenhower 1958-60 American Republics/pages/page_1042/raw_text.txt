Secretary Brucker is scheduled to meet early this week with his 
personnel to ascertain whether all plans for implementation of the pro-
gram have been completed. Assistant Secretary of the Army Roderick 
informed Mr. Stewart on April 4 that he still has not heard from the 
Department of Defense as to its capacity to award the ten per cent 
increase to non-United States rate employees of the military estab-
lishments in the Zone and the Canal Zone Government. Mr. Roderick 
expressed the view, concurred in by ARA, that the Panama Canal 
Company wage increase, already authorized by the Board of Directors, 
should not be granted unilaterally. He thought it would be preferable 
to hold up the press announcement, if necessary, until Defense and the 
Canal Zone Government could secure funds to enable them to grant 
the increase. ARA concurs, believing a much greater impact would be 
achieved by an all-inclusive wage increase for non-United States rate 
workers. Mr. Roderick said he was expecting momentarily to hear from 
Defense whether it had arranged for the necessary funds.
When all arrangements have been made to implement the nine
