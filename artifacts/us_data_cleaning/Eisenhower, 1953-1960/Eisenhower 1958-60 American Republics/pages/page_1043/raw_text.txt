Nine Point Program
The Secretary opened the meeting by expressing his delight at the 
nine point program for Panama which the Department of the Army had 
prepared and which was to be announced by means of a White House 
press release. He expressed the hope that the Department of Defense 
would be able to find the money for pay increases for Panamanian 
employees of the U.S. armed services. Mr. Roderick said that there was 
now no problem on this issue and that he was hopeful it would be 
resolved by the end of the week with the press announcement com-
ing out some time before April 15. He said the pay increases would 
go into effect on May 1. He also proposed a new sentence in the draft 
press release which would highlight the fact that this program was not 
an afterthought and reflective of submission to Panamanian pressure 
but was something which was in train before the November riots and 
would have been implemented before if the riots had not taken place. 
The Secretary agreed with this change with the addition of the thought 
that the delay was also caused by our own legislative procedures.2
