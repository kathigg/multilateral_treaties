Telegram from the Chargé in El Salvador (Downs) to the Department of State1  San Salvador, November 23, 1960, noon. 326. For Mann. Following from Stewart:2 After two hour meeting with Osorio, two hour session attended by all six members of Junta and about 15 interviews Salvadorans, Americans and others these are my observations: 1) Osorio was key figure in overthrow. He and civilian elements were in accord sometime before overthrow. 2) Osorio admits there will probably be counter attempts in armed forces and plotting is in evidence but based on my information Osorio has enough support in armed forces to keep present Government in power. Osorio has receded into background and provides occasional advice but three military members of Junta are his men and Minister Interior3 is strong type loyal to Osorio. Therefore, Osorio is key man in country. 3) Civilian members of Junta are well-­intentioned but inexperienced. I feel they are anxious to cooperate with U.S. If there were any doubt, however, Junta setup provides excellent check and balance with military members, backed by Osorio, exerting deciding influence.
