The Ambassador said it would appear desirable to him that any

sales should avoid attributing of this decision in any way to the IMF

other course would expose the Government to the charge of being a 
mere “puppet”.
Throughout the conversation both the President and Guevara 
showed a consistent determination to attack Bolivia’s critical problems 
with vigor and courage.

Washington, October 21, 1958.
[Subject:] Status of Stabilization Program in Bolivia.
The Government of Bolivia and a mission from the IMF reached 
agreement on a new stabilization program recently with the details 
contained in a letter dated October 4, 1958 to the Managing Director 
of the IMF.2 This letter was signed by the Finance Minister3 and the 
President of the Central Bank.4 It sets forth the stabilization policies 
and performance levels the government proposes to implement. It also 
requests reinstatement of Bolivia’s drawing rights under its stand-­by 
agreement with the Fund.5
