Colombia 377 This Country Team recommendation is based on our considered view that the violence problem is essentially a national problem which has developed to the extent that a military action by the armed forces is required. Thus, this is a military rather than a police problem; and suppression of violence is primarily a military task in which police action should be the secondary role of maintaining order in pacified areas and of participating in direct anti-­guerrilla action only where bandit activity is sporadic. Another consideration is that the Defense Department proposal is more responsive to President Lleras’ request, both as regards equipment and as regards reorientation of our military assistance program in general. In none of the numerous conferences between President Lleras and U.S. officials was it ever suggested that he had in mind the use of the National Police in connection with the requested assistance.
