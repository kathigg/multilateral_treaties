Letter from the Acting Secretary of State to the Secretary 
of the Army (Brucker)1
Washington, May 4, 1960.
Dear Mr. Secretary:
I refer to your letter of April 29 to the Secretary of State2 asking 
for the draft language of a proposed Executive Order concerning the 
status of our Ambassador to the Republic of Panama and the agenda 
of the other Panamanian topics for discussion. This was in connec-
tion with the meetings on Panama scheduled for May 10 and May 11.
As your office has already been informed by telephone, the Secretary 
has requested that these meetings be postponed until after the Summit3 
in view of the fact that he will have only three working days here between 
his return from Tehran and Istanbul and his departure for the Summit.
With respect to the Executive Order we have not completed our 
study but will of course send you a copy when this is done. It is my 
understanding that the suggested agenda will be along the lines of the 
Secretary’s letter to you of April 8,4 but we shall be in touch with you 
again after the meetings have been re-­scheduled.
Sincerely yours,
DOUGLAS DILLON
Acting Secretary

Editorial Note
On May 8, 1960, presidential elections were held in Panama to 
select a successor to President Ernesto de la Guardia, Jr., whose four-­
year term expired in the autumn. Dr. Roberto F. Chiari was elected the 
new President of Panama, and he assumed office on October 1, 1960. 
Documentation concerning the election campaign is in Department of 
State file 719.00.
