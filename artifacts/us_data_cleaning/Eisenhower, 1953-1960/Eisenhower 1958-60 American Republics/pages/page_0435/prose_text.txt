They feel, however, that the political situation will deteriorate rapidly if coffee prices drop much further and if economic prospects do not improve. Colombia had undergone quite a challenge with the return of Rojas Pinilla. People had not really expected him to return but the legal processes were being carried out now that he had. The trial will probably last another two or three weeks in the Senate and then he will be turned over to the Courts. Violence has abated in the worst areas of a year or so ago, but it has cropped up again in Caldas and northern Boyaca. Squatters are moving in on properties and are creating quite a serious problem. I stressed the role that Colombia has played traditionally in the OAS and in the UN and the great satisfaction which I took from seeing Colombia return to its outstanding position of the past. Diplomatic Asylum—The Foreign Minister said that Colombia is preparing to submit certain amendments to the convention on asylum for consideration at the Quito conference next year. They are in a difficult situation with Guatemala and Nicaragua, the embassies of both of these countries having given asylum to common criminals. They feel that the traditional rules of asylum should be respected and that the asylum-­granting country should determine when asylum may properly be granted, but the abuse must stop if the principle of asylum is to continue. One thing they have in mind recommending is that asylum should not be granted to military officers on active duty. CO–19.
