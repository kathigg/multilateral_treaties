President Eisenhower commented that those minor criticisms were 
nothing as compared with the ones that he has been receiving from Cuba, 
and he said that this situation poses a serious problem for the United 
States. Senator Batlle said that while Fidel CASTRO had great prestige 
throughout Latin America as a revolutionist, he had lost that prestige as a 
governor. He repeated this observation three times, wishing to emphasize 
it. In the course of emphasizing this point, he said that the countries of 
South America could not permit communism to gain control in Cuba. The 
President commented that this is really a problem for the Organization 
of American States, and the countries of the Western Hemisphere as a 
whole. After restarting his views in various ways, which appeared to be 
entirely critical of the Cuban Government, Senator Batlle departed.
