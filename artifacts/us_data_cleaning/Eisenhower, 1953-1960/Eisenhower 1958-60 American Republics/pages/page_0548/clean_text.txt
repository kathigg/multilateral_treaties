The crack-­down on Nicaraguans exiles who were engaged in plotting against Nicaragua and the Government’s threat to deport all exiles who engage in subversive activities in Costa Rica against another country are beneficial results of the unsuccessful invasion attempt. Despite press reports alleging that Costa Rican Government sources claimed Cuban collaboration with the Nicaraguan rebels based in Costa Rica there is no evidence to date of Cuban involvement or that Cuban arms were in the possession of the rebels. The Costa Rican Government is in full control of the border situation and there is little likelihood of a resumption of hostilities. The prompt and aggressive action by the Civil Guard should reassure the Nicaraguan Government that it can count on the Costa Rican Government for support in suppressing a small Costa Rican-­based aggressive revolutionary action against it.
