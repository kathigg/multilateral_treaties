I understand that the efforts of Peruvian leaders to improve Peru’s standard of living through encouraging the investment of local and foreign capital may suffer in an atmosphere of popular resentment against the United States. Peruvian leaders might more effectively point out that the United States has long advocated a multi-­lateral approach to the problem of excess lead and zinc production, as it has in the current coffee arrangement; that the United States Executive and many groups in the United States work constantly on behalf of free trade; and that the lead-­zinc market has strengthened considerably. You can be certain that my Government is very conscious of Peru’s interests in the products you have mentioned, and also in fishmeal exports that are becoming so important in Peru’s economy. President Eisenhower has been most disappointed at not being able to visit Peru, as he has told President Prado in a personal message.9 Your Ambassador has presented your attitude on this matter most effectively, and I can only emphasize that President Eisenhower’s decision was based on the limitations of time available for his trip.
