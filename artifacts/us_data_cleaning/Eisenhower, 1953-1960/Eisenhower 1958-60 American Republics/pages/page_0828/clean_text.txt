I would like to suggest that we consider conveying to President Duvalier the thought that the abolishment of the Secret Police, the release or trial of political prisoners, the restoration of the two suppressed newspapers to the status quo ante, and a halt to hounding of political opposition are prior conditions to our agreement to a Military Mission. There may be other conditions we may wish to include, but these are the main ones which occur to me now. If my recommendation is adopted, we will have to consider how to approach the President without upsetting our present friendly relations or arousing cries of “intervention”. Obviously it will be a difficult and delicate assignment. In closing, I wish to make it quite clear that I am heartily in favor of establishing a Military Mission here, preferably composed of members of the United States Marine Corps as requested by the Haitian Government. I can think of no surer guarantee of stability for the foreseeable future. However, I repeat, we should consider carefully whether we can justify the establishment of such a Mission to a Government responsible for the measures which I describe. Sincerely, Gerald A.
