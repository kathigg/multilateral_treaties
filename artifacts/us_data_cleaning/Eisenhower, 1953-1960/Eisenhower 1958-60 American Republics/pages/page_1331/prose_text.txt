Venezuela 1243 additional tax applied to only ½ of the excess income over the aggregate of the taxes. Companies whose net income after the normal and surtax did not exceed 10 percent of the capital invested were exempt from the additional tax. In practice the 50–50 principle has resulted in a somewhat greater share of revenues for the government than profits for the companies. It has been characteristic of the Venezuelan petroleum industry that a high volume of operations has been required before a profit could be realized. Therefore, until a level of production and profits has been reached allowing the 50–50 profit sharing tax to become effective, the division has resulted in favor of the government by reason of a relatively high royalty and heavy exploration and development costs.
