He said that he would have to proceed with great caution in the Seventh Meeting, for fear of touching off a revolution in Peru similar to the ones that had taken place in Bolivia or Cuba. Minister Porras said that he believed sincerely in a policy of cooperation with the United States and in the defence of Western principles, but that he would have to be careful not to support any measures that might incite the Cuban people to break Latin American unity. He said that Cuba had broken its ties with the United States by its violent attacks on the latter country, but that Cuba had no reason to break its ties with the other Latin American republics. He said that he hoped that Cuba would remain in the OAS for the sake of Latin American unity, and that Cuba would realize that the OAS represented the best safeguard of the Latin American countries. Minister Porras said that the world was divided into regional system, and that Cuba belonged in the inter-American system for historical, geographic, and cultural reasons. He said that Cuba, although it had insulted a number of Latin American countries, including his own, would not, in his opinion, dare to abandon the OAS.
