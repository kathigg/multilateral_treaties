Editorial Note
On March 24, 1958, in a Supplementary Report to the Report of the 
President’s Special Committee to Investigate Crude Oil Imports, dated July 
29, 1957, the Special Committee recommended additional restrictions 
on crude oil imports quotas for Districts I–IV, the area east of the 
Rocky Mountains. The President approved the Special Committee’s 
report in a memorandum dated March 25, 1958, and in similar memo-
randa released on June 4, July 1, and December 23, 1958, the President 
approved the Special Committee’s recommendations relating to 
1) importation of petroleum products other than crude, 2) allocations 
for District V, the Pacific West Coast, and 3) continuance of existing 
crude oil allocations through February 28, 1959. See Public Papers of the 
Presidents of the United States: Dwight D. Eisenhower, 1958 (Washington: 
1958), p. 230. Documentation concerning U.S. oil policy is printed in 
Foreign Relations, 1958–1960, vol. IV, Foreign Economic Policy. Further 
documentation is located in Department of State decimal files 411.006, 
411.316, and 831.2553, and in ARA/EST Files, Lot 61 D 319, “Petroleum 
Policy” and “Oil U.S. Legislation”, and ARA/EST Files, Lot 62 D 8, 
“Petroleum Policy” and “Oil Import Program.”
