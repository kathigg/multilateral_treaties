Summaries of Compilations LXXXV unwilling to concede that Cuba represented a hemispheric problem any more than did the Dominican Republic. (UR–14, 16) Nevertheless, the U.S.-Uruguayan relationship could only be described as very cooperative, in no small measure due to the change in government in Uruguay. The local economy, although somewhat stagnant, was basically prosperous. (UR–15) In the realm of politics, the United States felt that it could count on Uruguay’s support in the world arena. This was a soothing consideration, since that country maintained its longstanding reputation as the best small country in the Western Hemisphere: democratic, enlightened, and progressive. As the period drew to a close, the International Monetary Fund, with U.S. support, agreed to assist the Uruguayan economy by extending a $30 million loan. (UR–17)
