At this point, while photographers came in to take pictures, the 
conversation turned to the 9-man National Council of Government in 
Uruguay and Mr. Haedo jokingly suggested a similar system for the 
United States so that all of the candidates could become President for a 
year, as he himself would be doing next March 1.
Upon leaving at 11:30 a.m., the Councilor mentioned again his 
hopes that the President would enjoy the painting which he presented 
and he added that he would be most gratified if the President might 
find it possible to send him an autographed photograph and one of his 
own paintings. He reiterated his admiration for the President and his 
satisfaction at having been received by him. The President thanked him 
for making his friendly visit.

Editorial Note
On October 10, 1960, the International Monetary Fund announced 
that an initial par value for the Uruguayan peso had been estab-
lished at 7.40 pesos per United States dollar, by agreement between 
the Fund and the Government of Uruguay. The fund also announced 
that it had entered into a stand-­by agreement enabling the Uruguayan 
Government to draw the equivalent of United States $30,000,000 over a 
12-­month period. Documentation concerning IMF activities in Uruguay 
is contained in decimal file 833.10 and in ARA Files, Lot 62 D 359, “IMF.”
