Memorandum from the Under Secretary of State for Political Affairs (Merchant) to the President1 Washington, December 31, 1960. SUBJECT Determinations under Sections 105(b)(4) and 451(a) of the Mutual Security Act of 1954,2 as amended, permitting the furnishing of military assistance to Colombia The purpose of this memorandum is to recommend that you make the requisite determinations under Sections 105(b)(4) and 451(a) of the Mutual Security Act of 1954, as amended (hereinafter referred to as “the Act”), to permit the furnishing of military assistance to Colombia. The proposed military assistance is needed primarily to enable the Colombian Government to expand its campaign against organized bandits and guerrillas. For the past decade, these bandits and guerrillas have caused an appalling loss of life and destruction of property, and the capabilities of the Colombian Government to maintain internal security and to resist external aggression progressively have been weakened. Communist influence reportedly is increasing in the areas infested by bandits, and agents of the Castro Government in Cuba are known to be active among anti-­government groups.
