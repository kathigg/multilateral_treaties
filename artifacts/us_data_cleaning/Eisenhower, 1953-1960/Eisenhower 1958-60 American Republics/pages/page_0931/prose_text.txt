This, incidentally, brings up a point (7) which I should have included in the body of my letter in paragraph 2, i.e., the fact that Dr. Eisenhower is visiting Nicaragua should take some of the heat off Luis (and Guillermo) to have a visit to the U.S. While Guillermo hardly deserves credit for arranging the Eisenhower visit, although I believe he has spoken to Dr. Eisenhower several times about it, certainly no one else can take credit away from him for the idea. On the basis of my contact with Vice President Nixon and my knowledge of his tight schedule,5 Irving Davidson would have to pull something besides rabbits out of the hat to arrange for the V.P. to visit Managua.
