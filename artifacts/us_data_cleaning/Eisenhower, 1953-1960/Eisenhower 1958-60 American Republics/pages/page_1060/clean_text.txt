972 Foreign Relations, 1958–1960, Volume V, Supplement [Enclosure] SUGGESTED REPLY Dear Senator Green: Further to my letter of August fifteenth4 in reply to yours of August twelfth to the President, concerning the program for the improvement of relations between the United States and Panama which the President approved on April nineteenth, I am pleased to be able to inform you of the following actions which have been taken on each of the nine points contained in the program. Point 1. Provision was made for an increase effective May 1, 1960 of 10 percent in the wages of persons in the appropriate categories. Point 2. The apprentice training program was expanded by the appointment of 27 Panamanian apprentices in July 1960, two more than the number originally specified. Point 3. The construction of 500 housing units in the Canal Zone is scheduled in the Capital Budget program of the Panama Canal Company beginning in FY 1960. The program now developed provides for the construction of 100 units in each of the fiscal years 1961, 1962, 1963, and 1964. Twelve units were begun in FY 1960 and completion of the program is scheduled in FY 1965 with the construction of 88 apartments. Point 4. Meetings have been and are being held here to consider the various plans and proposals that have been developed for construction and financing of 500 housing units in Panama for Canal Zone employees. It is understood that the Development Loan Fund is presently entertaining a proposal for a guarantee to private capital interested in financing this project. Point 5. The Panama Canal Company has advertised for bids for the construction of a new water main and contracts will be let in the near future, following the opening of the bids. Point 6. Effective July 1 the rate at which water is sold to the Government of Panama for distribution in the cities of Panama and Colon was reduced from approximately [illegible] cents to 7.5 cents for the first 100,000 cubic feet and to 7 cents for additional units of this amount. Point 7. Legislation enacted by the United States Congress was signed by the President on July 14, 1960, granting $10.00 per month increase in cash relief payments effective as of July 1, 1960. Point 8. Salaries of teachers in the Canal Zone Latin American School system were increased 10 percent effective May 1, 1960. 4.
