Editorial Note Representatives of Costa Rica and the United States exchanged notes at Washington, March 4 and October 17, 1958, extending the Agreement establishing a Military Mission in Costa Rica for four years from December 10, 1957. For the text of these notes, see 11 UST (pt. 2) 2227. The Agreement was amended by notes exchanged at San José, February 25 and May 13, 1959, to bring Costa Rican payment procedures into conformity with United States law. For the text of the amendment, see 12 UST 901. The pertinent legislation is printed in 72 Stat. 275. CR–13. Editorial Note Airgram G–21 to San José, October 17, 1958, stated that the International Cooperation Administration was prepared to initiate a technical cooperation Public Safety Program in Costa Rica. (718.5/9– 3058) The Department of State and the International Cooperation Administration agreed to the Embassy’s suggestion, set forth in despatch 156 from San José, September 30, that it propose a study of internal security to the Costa Rican Government as a first step in starting such a program. (718.5/9–3058) In a letter of November 17 to President Echandi, Ambassador Willauer proposed a Costa Rican- United States study of the situation confronting the Costa Rican Public Forces. (Enclosure to despatch 266 from San José, November 19, 1958; 718.5/11–1958) No record of a reply to Willauer’s letter or of action on his proposal was found in Department of State files. CR–14.
