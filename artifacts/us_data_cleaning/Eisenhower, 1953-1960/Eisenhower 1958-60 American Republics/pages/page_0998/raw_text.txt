May I take this opportunity to reiterate the hope expressed in my 
letter of July twenty-­fifth, 1958,7 that adoption of these new employ-
ment and wage practices will make a significant contribution to the 
mutual respect and friendship existing between our Governments and 
peoples. I firmly believe that any differences existing with regard to the 
implementation of the new system can be resolved by patient discus-
sion and negotiation between your Government and the officials of the 
United States charged with the responsibility of administering it.
Please accept, Mr. President, my personal good wishes to you and 
to the people of Panama.8
Sincerely,
