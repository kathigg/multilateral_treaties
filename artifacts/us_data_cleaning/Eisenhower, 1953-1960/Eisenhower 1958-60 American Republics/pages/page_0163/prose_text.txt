As a consequence of these radical steps which were necessary to eliminate distortions in the economy, there was a sharp upward movement in prices during 1959 because of the depreciation of the exchange rate, the dropping of subsidy payments on basic articles of consumption, and the continued necessity to increase wage rates. As a result the cost of living increased by 75% during the first six months of 1959. However, once the initial adjustments in the price structure were made and the budgetary situation improved, there was a noticeable tendency toward price stability.
