I intend to discuss the foregoing with Generalissimo Trujillo in an endeavor to bring about his retirement in a manner which would insure a peaceful transition to democratic government. Naturally, I would refrain from such action if you did not desire it. 2. The foregoing plans and the implementation thereof are of importance to the United States. Therefore, I greatly desire to discuss these plans with the appropriate officials of your government, primarily Allen Dulles, prior to my departure for the Dominican Republic. Respectfully submitted, Edwin N. Clark DR–17. Memorandum of a Conference with President Eisenhower1 March 1960.
