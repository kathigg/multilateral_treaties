PE–31.	
Editorial Note
A controversy arose in Peru over possession by the Texas Petroleum 
Company in Peru of aerial photographs of Peru received from the United 
States Government without clearance from the Peruvian Government. 
The problem was taken up by the Peruvian Chamber of Deputies and 
publicized in the Peruvian press. The Peruvian Government made rep-
resentations to the American Embassy in Lima and fined the company. 
Documentation on these developments is in Department of State file 
723.022.
PE–32.	
Telegram from the Ambassador in Peru (Achilles) to the 
Department of State1

Lima, December 4, 1959, 1 p.m.
494. EMBTELS 485 and 487.2
In hour with President last night I told him U.S. like other govern-
ments much interested in his arms limitation proposal and had high 
hopes it could lead to concrete results.3 We therefore had difficulty in 
believing Berckemeyer’s report Peru had purchased second cruiser. 
One reason was categorical assurances we had received from GOP 
it would not be bought and we had not thought of doubting Peru’s 
word.4 Other was extremely adverse effect it would have on his arms 
limitation proposal and his international reputation which proposal 
had greatly enhanced.
