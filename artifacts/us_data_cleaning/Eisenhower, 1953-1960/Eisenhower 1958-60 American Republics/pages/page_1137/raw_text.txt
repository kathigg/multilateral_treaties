Letter from President Eisenhower to President Prado1
Newport, September 21, 1958.
Dear Mr. President:
Once again I consider it advisable to communicate with you 
directly on a matter of mutual concern. Following months of painstak-
ing effort to find a solution to the problem of the world oversupply 
of lead and zinc, I have felt obliged to take action now to relieve the 
genuinely distressed condition of this industry in the United States by 
establishing import quotas.2
Contrary to my hopes and expectations, the minerals stabiliza-
tion plan, which had been proposed as an alternative to accepting the 
recommendations of the United States Tariff Commission,3 although 
approved by the Senate, was defeated in the House of Representatives
