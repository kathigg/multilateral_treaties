Summaries of Compilations LXXVII Panama U.S. Relations With Panama Note: The parenthetical citations are to the numbered documents in the text U.S. relations with Panama during the final 3 years of the Eisenhower administration focused mainly on questions arising out of the administration of the Canal Zone, and upon the larger issue posed by the symbolic, but politically sensitive question of whether the Panamanian flag should be allowed to fly alongside the United States flag within the Canal Zone. For the Panamanian Government, the flag issue was one of burning domestic importance. There was no question of challenging U.S. control over the territory and operations of the Canal Zone, but there was a strong Panamanian desire to see recognition of Panamanian sovereignty over the territory of the Canal Zone by flying the Panamanian flag within the Canal Zone.
