By an exchange of notes at Bogotá, July 31, 1959 and January 11, 1960, the United States and Colombia entered into an agreement providing for a grant by the United States to Colombia for the acquisition of nuclear training and research equipment and materials. The agreement entered into force on January 11, 1960; for text, see 11 UST 138. On October 6, 1959, the United States and Colombia signed an exchange of notes at Bogotá, entered into force on the same date, constituting an agreement by which the United States would undertake to finance the sale to authorized Colombian purchasers for pesos certain surplus agricultural commodities produced in the United States, under the provisions of Title I of Public Law 480. For text of the agreement, see 10 UST (pt. 2) 1799. CO–7. Letter from the Assistant Secretary of State for Inter- American Affairs (Rubottom) to Milton S. Eisenhower1 Washington, April 9, 1958. Dear Dr. Eisenhower: I am rushing this letter to you by special delivery in the hope that you will receive it before you meet with the Colombian Foreign Minister.2 We anticipate that the Foreign Minister may take up some of the following matters with you: 1. Commercial Arrears. Colombia has accumulated a backlog of $470 million in commercial debts over the past four years or so, due in large part to careless financial policies of the previous administration. Last year arrangements were made to liquidate about $450 million of this through funding agreements, a credit of $60 million from the Eximbank, and other means. Colombia is finding it extremely difficult to meet payments due on this backlog and is hoping to obtain a long-­term loan to refund the commercial arrears debt.
