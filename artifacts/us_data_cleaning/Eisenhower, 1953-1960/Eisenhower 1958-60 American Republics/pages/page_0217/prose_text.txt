United States Regional Economic and Technical Assistance Policy 129 Sr. Bueso presented a memorandum2 on how the U.S. could help in the integration of Central American economies by the establishment of a Central American development bank. Such a bank could finance roads of common importance, the linking up of electric power networks, industrial loans of a regional character, and loans to enterprises requiring help because of readjustments of tariffs.
