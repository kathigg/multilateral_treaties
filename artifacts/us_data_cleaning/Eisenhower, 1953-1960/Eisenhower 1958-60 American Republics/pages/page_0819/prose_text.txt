Guatemala 731 I have this morning discussed the foregoing with Secretary Gates and the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff and they are agreeable to the suggested procedure. General Lemnitzer has indicated that ships now available in the Florida area could arrive off the Guatemalan coast within 48 hours. The ships available include an aircraft carrier and a small amphibious force with a contingent of Marines. It is my own feeling that given the possibility of leaks, it would be just as well not to have Marines aboard any of the surveilliance ships. General Lemnitzer points out the Marines would be merely on what is a routine and normal training [illegible in the original] in that area and that, should it be necessary to use the Marines, several days would be saved by having them aboard. I nevertheless feel it would be better to risk a delay than to risk the serious reaction which would inevitably follow should the rumor spread that Marines are en route to Gautemala.5 Christian A.
