In other words, the Fund contends that if aid is reduced by $5 million,

ment expenditures, and if such expenditures were financed through 
other means, severe inflationary pressure would result and the stabili-
zation program would be impaired.
7. ICA is not certain that the above analysis of the Fund with respect 
to the development sector is correct. Nevertheless it is prepared to agree 
(a) that counterpart deposits at the existing level of $83 billion bolivi-
anos should be sterilized (except that the need for sterilization at that 
level will be re-­examined periodically by USOM and IMF representa-
tives), and (b) that the additional $5 million should be drawn upon to the 
extent determined to be necessary by USOM to provide counterpart for 
agreed projects in the development sector, which may or may not reach 
117 billion bolivianos. It also recognizes that some or all of the additional 
$5 million may have to be provided in the form of a cash grant.
