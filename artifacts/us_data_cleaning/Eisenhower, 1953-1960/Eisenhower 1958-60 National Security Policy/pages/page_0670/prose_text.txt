In the postwar period the quality of Soviet scientific training has been high. Engineering training, while not as broad as that given an engineer in the West, is good within the particular field of special­ ization. Some deficiencies continue in the practical and experimental aspects of training, particularly in some fields of biology and engi­ neering. Recent changes in higher school curricula, intended to over­ come these deficiencies, include requirements for more laboratory and independent experimental work outside the classroom, as well as a plan to allow superior students to follow individual study schedules.
