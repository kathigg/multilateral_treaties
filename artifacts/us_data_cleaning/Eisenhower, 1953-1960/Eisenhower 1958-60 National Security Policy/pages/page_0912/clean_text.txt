Memorandum of Conference with the President1 Washington, August 4, 1959, 11 a.m. OTHERS PRESENT Dr. Kistiakowsky Major Eisenhower The President opened by asking Dr. Kistiakowsky if he had seen the memorandum from Secretary Gates to General Goodpaster, dated July 27th, on the subject of military department contracts for space activities. The President is primarily afraid of the develop­ ment of “squatters’ rights” by the individual services. He has asked Mr. McElroy to take a look at this subject, and also told Mr. McElroy that if Dr. York is not available to advise him, he should contact Dr. Kistiakowsky. He said that even $500,000 is a lot of money.
