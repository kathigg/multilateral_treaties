Interceptors—Secretary McElroy specified that in line with the cal­ culated rise of favoring nuclear retaliation over air defense, no pro­ grams for interceptors are being initiated in FY 1960. MACE—This missile, although an air breather, has not been dropped from the FY 1960 program because of its contributions to NATO. It has a good tactical range and carries a large warhead. Furthermore, it pro­ vides the only all-­weather capability in NATO against airfields. Mr. Quarles then made a brief reference to the relationships between the Department of Defense (ARPA) and the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA). In particular, he pointed out that the two agencies had joint research programs in solid propellants and in space activities. Finally, Mr. McElroy expressed his concern with the unemploy­ ment effects of program elimination. He pointed out, as an example, that the TITAN cancellation would have involved some 25, 000 people.
