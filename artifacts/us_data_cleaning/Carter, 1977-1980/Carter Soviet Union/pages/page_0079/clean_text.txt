Letter From President Carter to Soviet General Secretary Brezhnev1 Washington, March 24, 1977 To General Secretary Brezhnev The substantive answer to your recent letter2 will be presented in person by Secretary Vance. However, I wish to express my hope that his visit to Moscow will result in substantial general agreements regarding arms reduction and the enhancement of cooperation between our two countries as we search together for a more peaceful and friendly world. I hope to continue our correspondence after Mr. Vance’s return, and look forward to an early meeting with you. The Secretary of State has my complete confidence, he and I have spent hours together preparing for this important trip, and he is qualified to speak authoritatively on my behalf. You have my personal good wishes.
