Letter From President Carter to Professor Andrei Sakharov1
Washington, February 5, 1977
Dear Professor Sakharov:
I received your letter of January 21,2 and I want to express my ap-
preciation to you for bringing your thoughts to my personal attention.
Human rights is a central concern of my Administration. In my
inaugural address I stated: “Because we are free, we can never be indif-
ferent to the fate of freedom elsewhere.”3 You may rest assured that the
American people and our government will continue our firm commit-
ment to promote respect for human rights not only in our own country
but also abroad.
We shall use our good offices to seek the release of prisoners of
conscience, and we will continue our efforts to shape a world respon-
sive to human aspirations in which nations of differing cultures and
histories can live side by side in peace and justice.
I am always glad to hear from you, and I wish you well.
Sincerely,
Jimmy Carter
