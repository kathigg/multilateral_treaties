Against this background, I must tell you that neither our mutual
understanding nor our mutual security is served by recent Soviet state-
ments concerning this United States exercise. I would suggest that
when we meet in what I hope will be the near future we discuss ex-
changing information on our respective strategic exercises before they
take place with a view toward contributing to strategic stability. This
could be part of a broader discussion of the risk of nuclear war.
We both have a unique responsibility for dealing with this issue
which is of such fundamental importance to international security. It is
essential that we come to grips with it soon.
Sincerely,
Jimmy Carter
