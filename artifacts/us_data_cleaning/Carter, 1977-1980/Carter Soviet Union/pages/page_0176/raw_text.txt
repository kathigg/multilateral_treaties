Memorandum of Conversation1
Washington, June 23, 1977, 12:40–2:50 p.m.
PARTICIPANTS
Zbigniew Brzezinski, Assistant to the President for National Security Affairs
Anatoliy Dobrynin, Ambassador of the Soviet Union
I had lunch today (12:40–2:50) with Ambassador Dobrynin, on his
initiative. The luncheon was a compromise-picnic: I provided the
White House meal and he provided vodka and caviar, a bargain that I
felt was equitable to both sides.
After usual preliminaries, we agreed that we would not talk about
specific issues in any negotiating fashion but would simply discuss in
broad terms the state of U.S.-Soviet relations. He asked me for my
opinion and in my opening comments, I essentially suggested the
following:
1. Our relationship is approaching a key phase in regards to some
central issues, notably SALT, and, in some measure also, the Middle
East. On SALT, I expressed concern that the pace of negotiations may
not be rapid enough and that we may be faced by early fall with a situa-
tion which at least our publics will interpret as involving a crisis. This
