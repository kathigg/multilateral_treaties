5. Gromyko’s “question” was proceeded by his usual colorful re-

SALT, and I told him we were already being firm. He accused us of
seeking unilateral advantages, and I responded that it was the Soviets
who were doing just that and that our proposals were fair. He was pre-
dictably sour about our new types proposal that I made this morning,
saying that it was simply a ruse to allow us to continue our own pro-

bridge the gap, taking into account the national interests of both sides. I
believe that the Soviets have not had time to give it any serious
