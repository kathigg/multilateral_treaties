small unit raise in terms of threatening the security of the United
States? Gromyko was sure that he was right in saying that if this matter
were viewed objectively, none of the arguments made on the US side
would stand up.
The Secretary said he had nothing further to add, except to point
out that he had not spoken of seeing fewer Cubans, but no Cubans at
all. He said that he would report this conversation to his President and
hoped Gromyko would do the same with respect to President
Brezhnev. Gromyko said that he would naturally do so.
Both the Secretary and Gromyko noted that they would meet again
