Secretary’s Speech: Ambassador Dobrynin commented favorably on
the Secretary’s speech in Los Angeles.2 He characterized it as a con-
structive speech.
PNG Exchange: The Secretary expressed concern that the US and
the USSR might be headed toward a serious spiral of actions which
could affect our general relationship, and reviewed the steps we would
be obliged to take if the Soviets did not reconsider their action against
our number three man in the commercial office in Moscow. The Secre-
tary generally followed the talking points (attached)3 and added with
some feeling that it was unfortunate that both countries were getting
involved in a series of steps which were disturbing. This was childish
and would not be conducive to good relations. He said he would like to
see us “knock it off.” Relations were too important to have endangered.
Dobrynin began his reply by referring to the two cases in Moscow. He
said the USSR didn’t want to make a public fuss about it, but they were
good cases. At the time of the first one, he was in Moscow and saw the
documentations and he also was well informed of the second one. He
said that it related to very sensitive matters in the foreign ministry itself
