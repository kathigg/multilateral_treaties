Covert and Overt Operations 308. Editorial Note In early July 1980, the impending death of the Shah and whether the United States should have an official presence at his funeral, as well as the kind of statement the United States should issue on his death, became an issue of discussion between the Department of State and the National Security Council Staff. Zbigniew Brzezinski, the President’s Assistant for National Security Affairs, wrote President Jimmy Carter a July 2 note advising that he should be aware of the draft telegram with instructions to U.S. Ambassador to Egypt Alfred (Roy) Atherton (see footnote 3, Document 303). Brzezinski suggested that the United States not send an official delegation to the funeral, but that Atherton attend the funeral. Carter approved these points, writing “ok” beside the first point, and “I agree” in the right margin beside the second. Carter also approved contingency draft letters of condolence.
