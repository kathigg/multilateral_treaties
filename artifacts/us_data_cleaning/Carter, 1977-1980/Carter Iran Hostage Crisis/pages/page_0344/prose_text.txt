Memorandum From the President’s Assistant for National Security Affairs (Brzezinski) to President Carter1 Washington, December 21, 1979 SUBJECT NSC Weekly Report #122 1. Opinion Difficult Choices in Iran. Let me just add the following to our discussions this morning,2 because I know that this matter is very much on your mind. (1) It may well be that Khomeini cannot be moved by economic pressures in which case military action, which is merely an extension of economic pressure (blockade or mining), will not move him either. In the meantime such action could provoke widespread international reactions against us and thus be self-defeating. Because of that, we need to consider military actions which contribute to his downfall, and thus secure the release of hostages as a consequence of attaining the other objective: his downfall.
