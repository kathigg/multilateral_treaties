Human Rights Memorandum From the Assistant Secretary of the Treasury for International Affairs (Bergsten) to the Deputy Secretary of State (Christopher)1 Washington, June 9, 1977 SUBJECT Human Rights and the IFIs From our experience thus far I strongly believe we need the following to improve the process by which we make human rights decisions in connection with IFI loan proposals: 1. timely written reports on the human rights situation in the borrowing countries in question, 2. specific criteria for judging performance and changes in the human rights situation in offending countries, and 3. a system for coordinating with other countries concerning U.S. action on human rights and foreign assistance. Concerning (1), above, the recent case of Benin made it clear that we need up-to-date reports on the status of human rights conditions before making a decision on how the United States should vote, and on any other necessary actions.
