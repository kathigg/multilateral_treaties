World Bank Group lending expressly for population and nutrition in 1979 amounted to more than $114 million, an almost threefold increase over the level of $47 million achieved in 1977. In addition, the World Bank now incorporates family planning components into some of their other lending programs including those for health and rural development. The regional development banks intend to do the same. In health we are carrying forward your commitment, first announced in May 1978 and reiterated by Mrs. Carter at the World Health Organization in May 1979, to promote health in the Third World.9 Drawing on your Statement of Principles,10 we are expanding support for primary health care and clean water/sanitation, and for control of major diseases such as malaria.
