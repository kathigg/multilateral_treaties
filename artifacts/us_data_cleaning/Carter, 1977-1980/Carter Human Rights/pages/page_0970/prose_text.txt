World Hunger and Food Policy 273. Memorandum From the Special Representative for Economic Summits (Owen) to President Carter1 Washington, undated SUBJECT World Hunger Aspect of Your Rome Visit Bob Bergland and Tom Ehrlich propose (Tab A)2 that you take the opportunity of your visit to Rome to call public attention to the advanced countries’ responsibilities and the United States’ good record in the War on Hunger. They suggest that you either make televised remarks to the four UN food agencies headquartered in Rome, or issue a press statement on this theme during your visit.3 I offered the former idea during early planning for your visit, but it got nowhere.4 I now suggest a different approach, serving the same purpose as Bergland and Ehrlich have in mind, but more likely to get media coverage here and requiring no change in your visit schedule.
