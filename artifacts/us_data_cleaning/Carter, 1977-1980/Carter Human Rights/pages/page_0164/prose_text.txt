Human Rights Memorandum From Secretary of State Vance to President Carter1 Washington, April 15, 1977 SUBJECT International Financial Institutions Authorization Bill Summary The Senate Foreign Relations Committee will mark-up the international financial institutions (“IFI’s”) authorization bill on April 19. The issue for your decision is whether we should (a) vigorously support the human rights language reported by Senator Humphrey’s Subcommittee on Foreign Assistance and (b) oppose the Badillo Amendment which requires the U.S. to vote against any loan to countries where human rights are violated. I recommend that we do so, and make your views known through a letter to Humphrey (draft at Tab 1).2 The recommended approach has been approved by Humphrey. Background On April 2, 1977, you wrote Congressman Henry Reuss a letter applauding his Committee’s action in adding a human rights title to legislation authorizing U.S. participation in the IFI’s.3 As you will recall, the Reuss approach provided broader discretion than the restrictive Harkin Amendment.
