Yugoslavia As the meeting was about to break up, Mr. Kolisevski asked if it would be possible to get satellite shots of mineral and water resources in Yugoslavia, especially since the United States had given such photographs to Bulgaria. (U) President Carter agreed, noting that he had given a book of photographs to President Tito during his visit in March 1978 and told him that if he needed more, the United States could supply them. The President said it would be easy for us to supply such photographs, and we would be glad to do it. (U) 296. Memorandum of Conversation1
