(National Archives, RG 59, Central Foreign Policy File, D773089–0398) On October 22, during a speech in Los Angeles at a Democratic National Committee fundraiser, President Carter told the audience that the recent conviction of Czechoslovak dissidents created “serious problems” in the relationship between the two countries. (Public Papers: Carter, 1977, Book II, p. 1896) During a White House press conference on October 27, President Carter responded to a question about South Africa’s human rights violations by once again referring to Czechoslovakia’s conviction of dissidents. Carter suggested that trade policy and cultural exchanges could be curtailed when another country violated policies that were important to the United States.
