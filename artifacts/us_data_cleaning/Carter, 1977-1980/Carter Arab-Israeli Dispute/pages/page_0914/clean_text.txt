March 27–December 31, 1979 879 In Vienna I raised with President Brezhnev the issue of renewal of the UNEF mandate.7 Our discussion was inconclusive. Though we will not be totally sure of the final Soviet position until the Security Council meets, I am not now encouraged that the USSR can be brought to cooperate with us. We have under study your concept of an all-African force. Whether it is feasible as an alternative has to be considered carefully. Our two governments and Israel will have to consult closely on a common strategy. With regard to the peace process, Bob Strauss will be discussing the critical issues with you.8 He will speak for me and with full authority. We are determined to make progress, and I look forward to receiving his report when he returns. With my warmest best wishes, Sincerely, Jimmy Carter, Unquote.
