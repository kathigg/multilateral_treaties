1384 Foreign Relations, 1977–1980, Volume IX Following President Navon’s state visit to Cairo in October, 1980, Egypt and Israel decided to establish a “peace task force” drawn primarily from the private sector to improve mutual understanding. The two countries are now considering proposals to open consulates and cultural centers to support their diplomatic missions. Both Egypt and Israel have continued to monitor the progress of the normalization of relations, and when problems have developed they have quickly resolved the problems through normal diplomatic channels. This record of achievement and progress stands out as a dramatic example to all the countries and peoples of the Middle East of the benefits of genuine peace. Respectfully submitted, Sol M.
