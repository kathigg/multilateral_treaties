Summary of a Telephone Conversation Between President Carter and Egyptian President Sadat1 Washington, May 13, 1980 The President called President Sadat at 0605 EST this morning and asked about Sadat’s speech to Parliament. The Egyptian president indicated he had just finished speaking and that “everything will be okay.” Sadat confirmed the President’s statement that Ali2 would now be doing most of the negotiating. When asked about resumption of the talks, Sadat said he would tell Parliament tomorrow3 that because of the President’s “interference” they will resume next week. (C) The President informed Sadat that his daughter-in-law was doing fine and Sadat expressed his and his son’s great appreciation for what the President is doing in that regard.
