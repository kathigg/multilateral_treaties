I. Introduction
(TS) National Security Decision Memorandum Number 242, with 
its call for the discriminate and controlled use of nuclear weapons, first 
advanced the concept of a secure reserve force—­a survivable, strate-
gic force held in reserve for “protection and coercion during and after 
nuclear conflict.”2 In addition, PD/NSC–18 directed that “U.S. target 
plans should provide for the maintenance of a secure reserve force to 
be withheld for possible use subsequent to a major nuclear exchange.”3
(TS) The “strategic reserve” forces presently consist of: (1) the 
Secure Reserve Force, (2) withheld forces, (3) residual forces, and (4) 
recovered and reconstituted forces. This study deals only with the 
Secure Reserve Force (SRF).
