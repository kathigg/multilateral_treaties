6. Follow-­on goals.
The major follow-­on concern is keeping the IVORY ITEM subject 
on the agenda. As the President reacts, it is clear what steps to take. If he 
never addresses the IVORY ITEM and WHEP contingencies, we simply 
cannot know where he wants to go in future developments.
7. The next stage.
The concept of NCA interaction with the military planners for 
nuclear “crisis management,” as set down in NSDM 242, is sound.
Any new PD emerging from the PD–18 follow-­on studies should 
retain it. I am preparing a separate memorandum on this point, one 
based on the President’s guidance on the Defense memorandum of 
August 19. I do not believe we should rush ahead with it now but rather 
let the IVORY ITEM discussions take their course. They are providing 
the interaction which eventually we might want to routinize in a more 
formal sense.
