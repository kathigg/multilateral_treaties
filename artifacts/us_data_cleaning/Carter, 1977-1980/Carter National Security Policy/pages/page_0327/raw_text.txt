Another alternative is the Ground-Launched Cruise Missile 
(GLCM) for which we currently have very tentative plans for initial 
deployment in 1982. Those plans involve deployment of 500 to 600 
missiles, each with a variable yield warhead up to [less than 1 line not 
declassified]. If we have to respond quickly to a rapid Soviet buildup in 
total RVs, this program, or a similar one involving surface-­ship launch, 
could be quickly and greatly expanded. The production rate can be rap-
idly multiplied, and is not restricted by SALT after the Protocol expires 
(though it could pose other political problems).
In sum, we must be aware that the Soviets could largely negate 
our lead in warheads by 1985. However, there are SALT-­permitted 
options available to maintain our lead in strategic warheads, if we 
so desire.
