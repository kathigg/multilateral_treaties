Prospects The average annual growth rate of 4 to 5 percent implied by these estimates exceeds the average annual rate at which we expect the Soviet economy to grow in the years ahead. Econometric projections are that Soviet GNP will grow at about 4 percent a year through 1980, but that growth will fall off thereafter—­probably to about 3 to 3.5 percent. Yet, the projected economic slowdown notwithstanding, Soviet spending for defense is likely to continue to grow at roughly its current rate into the 1980s, whether or not a SALT II agreement is concluded.
