5. State officials did not participate in the translation of the doc-
trine into specific SIOP instructions and for very good reasons. These 
adjustments are extraordinarily sensitive. The tradition of keeping mil-
itary contingency planning away from our diplomatists except in its 
general outline is an old and valid one. (S)
The whole thing seems to me more a matter of personal sensitivity 
than substance, even though Brown was somewhat slow in briefing 
Muskie after you initiated that approach; however, both of them were 
on the move and it was not easy for them to get together. Nevertheless, 
the central point remains valid: the SIOP should not be reviewed or 
discussed by those not authorized or required to deal with it; only the 
broad doctrine should be—­and Harold’s statements of broad doctrine 
are—­on the public record. (S)
