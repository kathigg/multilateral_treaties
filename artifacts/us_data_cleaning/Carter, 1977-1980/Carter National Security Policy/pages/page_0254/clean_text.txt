By the early 1990s, both the US and the Soviet Union could develop a capability for the short range point defense of ships, ground targets and aircraft. This capability could include structural as well as sensor kill devices. A US or Soviet space-­ based laser defense capability could defend friendly satellites against ASAT attack by the early 1990s. But development and deployment of such a capability in this time frame will involve significant risk. It is not likely that either the US or the Soviet Union will develop an effective laser ABM capability by the early 1990s.
