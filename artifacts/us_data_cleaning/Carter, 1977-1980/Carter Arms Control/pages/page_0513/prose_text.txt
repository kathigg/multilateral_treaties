Presidential Directive/NSC–381 Washington, May 20, 1978 TO The Vice President The Secretary of State The Secretary of Defense ALSO The Secretary of Energy The Director, Arms Control & Disarmament Agency The Chairman, Joint Chiefs of Staff The Director of Central Intelligence The Director, Office of Science and Technology Policy SUBJECT Comprehensive Test Ban (C) The President has reviewed the recent deliberations of the SCC on the Comprehensive Test Ban (CTB) issues, and has reached the following conclusions. a. In view of the importance of maintaining confidence in safety and reliability of our stockpiled nuclear weapons, the US should propose in the CTB negotiations that the treaty have a fixed, five year duration. The treaty would automatically terminate at the end of five years. During the fifth year there would be a review conference to determine whether to negotiate a replacement treaty.
