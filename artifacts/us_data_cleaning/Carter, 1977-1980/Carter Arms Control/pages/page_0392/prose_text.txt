Comprehensive Test Ban 154. Memorandum From the Director of the Arms Control and Disarmament Agency (Warnke) to the President’s Assistant for National Security Affairs (Brzezinski)1 Washington, April 29, 1977 SUBJECT Proposed Underground US Nuclear Weapons Test Program for the Second Half of Fiscal 1977 With regard to the test program proposed by ERDA2 for the second half of Fiscal 1977, I recommend: 1. a review of the present US policy for tests near the 150 kiloton limit established by the Treaty on the Limitation of Underground Nuclear Weapon Tests,3 and deferral of a decision regarding REBLO- CHON and SCANTLING—the two proposed tests near this limit— pending the completion of such a review. The current policy of allowing tests at design yields of up to 150 kilotons in face of the actual yield limit of 150 kilotons specified by the Treaty is defective because it allows tests with an unacceptably high risk of violating the Treaty. A more prudent approach should be adopted. The best approach appears to be limiting tests to devices having a maximum credible underground yield no greater than 150 kilotons; 2. an interagency study of the military utility and the arms control implications of developing and deploying nuclear weapons with insertable nuclear components, and, pending completion and review of this study, deferral of a decision on the conduct of the KNIGHTHEAD test in support of such development.
