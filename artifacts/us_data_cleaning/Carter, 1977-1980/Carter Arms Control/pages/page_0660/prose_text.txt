Comprehensive Test Ban 257. Editorial Note The final negotiating session among the United States, the United Kingdom, and the Soviet Union on a comprehensive test ban during the administration of President Jimmy Carter lasted from October 6 to November 14, 1980. Department of State negotiators noted the “progress” that had been made over the previous 42 months, but acknowledged that issues such as the duration of a CTB treaty, the issue of National Seismic Stations, and on-site inspections still needed to be resolved.
