Telegram From the Embassy in Pakistan to the Department
of State1
Islamabad, September 25, 1978, 1010Z
9262. Subject: Assessment of Evolving GOP Attitudes/Policy
Toward Afghanistan. Ref: (A) State 240411 (Notal), (B) Kabul 7370,
(C) Tehran 8927 (Notal), (D) Islamabad 8841, (E) Islamabad A–98
(Notal).2
1. Pakistan’s attitudes, and consequently its policies toward the
Democratic Republic of Afghanistan (DRA) are in the process of subtle
revision. The state of shock and alarm which we found (and reported)
during the spring, in the immediate aftermath of the Kabul coup,3 is
giving way to a more measured assessment and concern over the long-
term threat. Indeed, it would have been difficult to sustain the fever
pitch of May and June in the absence of direct Afghan (or Pakistani)
provocation.
