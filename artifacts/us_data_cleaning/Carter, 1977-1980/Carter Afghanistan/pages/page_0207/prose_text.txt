(S) Editorial Note In August 1979, the Central Intelligence Agency issued a number of reports assessing Soviet intentions with regard to Afghanistan and the increasingly precarious position of the Afghan Government. While stopping short of predicting with certitude the ultimate collapse of the Taraki-Amin regime and consequent military intervention by the Soviet Union, intelligence reporting noted two fundamental and interrelated trends: the Afghan Government was weakening in the face of the growing rebel insurgency, and in response the USSR was steadily increasing its efforts to equip and train the Afghan military in an attempt to contain and ultimately defeat the insurgency.
