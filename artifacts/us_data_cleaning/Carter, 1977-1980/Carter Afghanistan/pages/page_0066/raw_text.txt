1. IMPLICATIONS FOR MOSCOW OF THE AFGHAN COUP
The Soviets almost certainly are ambivalent about the coming to
power of a seemingly pro-Soviet government in Kabul headed by a
known Communist because of the damage it is likely to do to the USSR’s
position in Iran and Pakistan, and possibly in India. Nevertheless, the
Soviets apparently are doing what they can to improve its ability to
remain in power, in hopes of securing greater influence with it.
The Soviets welcome the advent of a leftist government in Kabul,
but they know its Afghan Communist component has very little sup-
port domestically. Their major concern over the short-term is that the
new government’s naming of Communists to top positions will not
only galvanize local opposition but perhaps also invite outside med-
dling. They also recognize that, in any event, there almost certainly
will be severe complications in their own relations with other neighbor-
ing states.
