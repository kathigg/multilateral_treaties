I do not think that in the near future Amin realistically expects any substantial forward movement in U.S.-Afghan relations, such as the resumption of aid. My guess is that he will be content for the time being with the less rancorous discourse we are having with his government, agreeing to avoid confrontational actions provided we do the same. In this connection he seconded with some alacrity my observations that sometimes two countries can work toward a better relationship simply by refraining from doing each other mischief. Perhaps, this is all we should strive for, as we assess Amin’s staying power both vis-à-vis the Soviets and the insurgents and as we try to maintain a limited presence toward the time when there might once again be some scope for a larger and more active U.S. role in Afghanistan.
