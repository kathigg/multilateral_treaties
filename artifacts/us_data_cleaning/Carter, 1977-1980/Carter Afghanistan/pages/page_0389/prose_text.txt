We have no credible evidence, however, that this was not a collective decision wholeheartedly adopted by the entire Politburo. Brezhnev has been active at least on-and-off of late, and so far as we know the ailing Kosygin is the only member of the top leadership who would have been unable to take part in planning for the Afghan operation. 10. If our theory is correct, then along with the Afghan decision the leadership must also have taken some basic decisions on such matters as détente and the shape of U.S.-Soviet relations in the immediate future. While we are not prepared as yet to conclude that this represents a permanent Soviet turn away from the policy of détente, it rather plainly suggests that Moscow does not expect any real progress in relations with the U.S. in the months ahead.
