Memorandum From the Special Representative for Economic Summits (Owen) to President Carter1 Washington, November 30, 1978 SUBJECT Your Aid Query At last week’s budget meeting,2 you asked what the increases in US concessional economic assistance had been from FY 1977 to FY 1978, and from FY 1978 to FY 1979—and how this related to our published outyear aid goals. 1. Increases. In FY 1977 the Congress provided concessional economic assistance programs totalling $5,718 million and in FY 1978 $6,524 million—an increase of about 14% in nominal terms, or about 7% in real terms. In FY 1979 the Congress provided $6,945 million in concessional economic assistance, or an increase of 7% in nominal terms, which means no increase in real terms. 2. Goals. In November 1977, you decided that total concessional aid should rise by one-third in real terms between FY 1978 and FY 1982.3 As you can see, we achieved none of this rise from FY 1978 to FY 1979. 3. Policy. I do not argue that FY 1980 should see a large aid increase. Having been one of the first to press for deep budget cuts in the EPG, I would be embarrassed to do so.
