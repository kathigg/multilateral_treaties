If we should fail to gain an extension of the waiver, there is one other option for avoiding collection of the duties: Treasury could reach deep into its bag of administrative tricks and come up with a way of merely “suspending liquidation” of the duties, although technically the authority does not really exist. This would be an act of desperation which could compound the problem, i.e., damage our credibility abroad (we’ve been saying we have no option but to collect the duties January 3, 1979) and possibly enrage elements of Congress. Finally, you should be reminded that Murray Finley has thirteen countervailing duty complaints pending at Treasury involving approximately $1 billion worth of textile products from less developed countries.
