Memorandum From the Special Representative for Economic Summits (Owen) to President Carter1 Washington, May 27, 1980 SUBJECT Summit Preparations (U) I have just returned from the second Summit Preparatory Meeting; economic preparations are in good shape, although there are still some disagreements. (C) 1. The draft communique’s section on macro-economic policy stresses the need for (i) prolonged fiscal and monetary restraint, to dampen inflationary expectations; (ii) policies that will encourage investment by shifting resources from the public to the private sector, from consumption to investment, and from declining to growing industries. This is the most important part of the Summit as far as Mrs. Thatcher is concerned. She believes that this kind of hortatory language will help heads of government by showing that these painful policies are regarded by all major industrial countries as the right recipe. (C) 2. The energy section includes strong commitments regarding increased production of alternative energy sources, particularly coal and synthetics.
