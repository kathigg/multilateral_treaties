Foreign Relations, 1977–1980, Volume III In the early 1970s the Japanese began shipping cargo boxes and cab chassis separately for assembly in the U.S. to take advantage of the low 4 percent duty on cab chassis. Customs found this practice acceptable for about 10 years until a recent court decision caused it to reconsider. In making its decision to reclassify lightweight cab chassis as substantially complete trucks, Customs noted that U.S. assembly involved less than 3 percent of the parts and 7 percent of the value of the completed trucks.
