International Monetary and Trade Policy, 1980–1981 231. Memorandum From the United States Trade Representative (Askew) to President Carter1 Washington, February 8, 1980 SUBJECT Nonrubber Footwear Imports I. BACKGROUND Following a U.S. International Trade Commission (USITC) finding of serious injury to the domestic nonrubber footwear industry in 1977, you ordered negotiation of orderly marketing agreements (OMAs) and domestic measures to enable the industry to adjust to import competition.2 In so doing, you rejected the Commission’s recommendation for an additional high duty on imports over 266 million pairs as not fairly balancing your concerns for domestic jobs and production, inflation, and expanded world trade. You stated that you were reluctant to include import restraints in your remedy but had done so in view of the serious situation in the domestic footwear industry.
