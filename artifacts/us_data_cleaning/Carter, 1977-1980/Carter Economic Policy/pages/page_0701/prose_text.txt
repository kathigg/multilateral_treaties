Foreign Relations, 1977–1980, Volume III 230. Memorandum From the Special Representative for Economic Summits (Owen) to President Carter1 Washington, January 18, 1980 SUBJECT Reduction of Disincentives to US Exports (U) The study of export disincentives pursuant to your September 1978 export policy decision2 has now been underway for a year. This memorandum summarizes findings and proposed phased decisions, beginning with interim measures that you might announce next week, along with good news about exports and the balance of payments. (U) The study has focused on our trade with non-Communist countries; issues peculiar to East-West trade were not included in the study or in the decisions proposed below. (U) Progress in carrying out this study has been slow, largely because of the difficulty of securing reliable data. The task force conducting the study has estimated that all the indentified disincentives may result in a combined annual export loss of $5–10 billion, but these figures cannot be supported by firm evidence. (C) The five major disincentives are: —ambiguities in the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act3 —overlapping anti-boycott laws and regulations —foreign policy export controls —nuclear export controls —arms export controls.
