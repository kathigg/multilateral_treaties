Memorandum From the Special Representative for Economic Summits (Owen) to President Carter1 Washington, June 15, 1978 SUBJECT Legislative Constraints on Foreign Assistance Secretary Vance’s memo at Tab A, prepared at your request,2 transmits a detailed list (Tab 1) of existing legislative constraints on our authority to carry out U.S. military and economic assistance programs and a list of proposed renewals or new constraints (Tab 2).3 He also recommends actions to deal with these restraints. This list offers persuasive evidence that your complaint about legislative interference is well founded. Secretary Vance points out that the restrictions fall into three general categories: 1. Conditional Restrictions. These are provisions that allow certain actions to be taken only if it is first determined that specified criteria are met and that the Congress is so informed. 2. Prohibitions. These prevent the granting of assistance by naming countries explicitly or by setting forth criteria that trigger such action automatically. 3. Congressional Veto.
