International Monetary and Trade Policy, 1979 227. Memorandum From Secretary of the Treasury Miller to President Carter1 Washington, September 26, 1979 The dollar came under very great pressure today and we had to spend almost half a billion dollars. In my view, only much more effective cooperation from the German monetary authorities will enable us to stabilize the market.2 I would like to have my discussions in Hamburg with the Germans this Saturday3 reinforced by a telephone call from you to Chancellor Schmidt along the lines of the attached talking points.4 1 Source: Carter Library, National Security Affairs, Brzezinski Material, Agency
