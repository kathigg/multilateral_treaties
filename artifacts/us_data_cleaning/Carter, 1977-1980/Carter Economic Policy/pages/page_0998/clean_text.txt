North-South Issues; Commodities Policy, 1978 963 If you approve my recommendations, our FY 82 appropriations level will be about $4.3 billion compared to our current level of $3.5 billion (a base of $2.7 billion with $835 million in arrearages). If we are successful in eliminating the need to appropriate callable capital, the FY 82 level would be only around $2.0 billion, compared to $1.6 billion in FY 79 on that basis. Congressional support for these figures will be difficult to achieve and will require a maximum political effort by the Administration each year. However, if successful, there will be major benefits for our international economic policy and for overall U.S. foreign policy. W.
