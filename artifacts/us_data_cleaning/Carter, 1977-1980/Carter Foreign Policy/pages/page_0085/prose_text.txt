We also regret our government’s continuing failure to oppose the denial of human freedom in Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union. The Republican Administration with the Sonnenfeldt statement,5 has shown a lack of sensitivity to the craving of the Eastern European people for greater independence. That is unacceptable. Only 13 months ago, President Ford and Henry Kissinger traveled to Helsinki to sign the treaty of comprehensive security and cooperation in Europe.6 It was supposed to lead to greater personal freedom for the peoples of Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union, including greater freedom to travel, to marry, and to emigrate.
