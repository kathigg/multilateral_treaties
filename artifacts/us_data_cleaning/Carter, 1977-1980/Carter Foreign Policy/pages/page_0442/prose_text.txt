Remarks by President Carter1 Washington, May 30, 1978 Mr. President, Mr. Secretary General,2 Excellencies, members of the Council, and distinguished guests: On behalf of the people of the United States, I welcome here today our closest friends and allies, the leaders of the North Atlantic Alliance. Twenty-nine years ago, at an uncertain time for world peace, President Truman spoke these words on signing the North Atlantic Treaty, and I quote from him: “In this pact, we hope to create a shield against aggression . . . a bulwark which will permit us to get on with the real business of government and society, the business of achieving a fuller and a happier life for all our citizens.”3
