Memorandum From the President’s Chief Speechwriter (Fallows) to President Carter1 Washington, May 10, 1978 SUBJECT Naval Academy Speech I understand from Fran2 that you have agreed to speak at the Naval Academy on June 7. So that we can begin our consultations and research as soon as possible, it would help me to know whether you have any subject in mind. Jody has mentioned one theme that sounds promising to me—a discussion of the role of the military now, after the time when they took so much abuse, and were so wounded in spirit, during Vietnam. Not only would that theme be appropriate for the audience, but also it could enable you to sound firm as at Wake Forest,3 when discussing the relevance of the military ideal in this generation.
